Bhikkhu Vibhanga - Nissaggiya Pācittiya 15: The Training Rule on Sitting Cloths.

  • Translated from the Pali by Bhikkhu Brahmali.   (More copyright information)

Origin story

1At one time the Buddha, the Master, was staying at Sāvatthī in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. There the Master addressed the monks: “Monks, I wish to go into solitary retreat for three months. No one is to approach me except the one who brings me almsfood."

“Yes, Master," they replied, and no one approached him except the one who brought him almsfood.

At that time the Saṅgha of Sāvatthī made the following agreement: “The Master wishes to be on solitary retreat for three months. No one is to approach the Master except the one who brings him almsfood. Anyone who approaches the Master should be made to confess a pācittiya offence."

2Just then Venerable Upasena Vaṅgantaputta and his followers approached the Master, paid homage to him and sat down to one side. It is the custom for Buddhas to greet visiting monks, and so the Master said this to Venerable Upasena: “I hope you are keeping well, Upasena, I hope you are comfortable; I hope you are not tired from travelling."

“We are keeping well, Master, we are comfortable; we are not tired from travelling."

3One of Venerable Upasena’s students was seated not far from the Master, and the Master said to him: “Do you like rag-robes, monk?"

“I don’t like rag-robes, bhante."

“Why then do you wear them?"

“My preceptor wears them, and so I do it too."

Then the Master said this to Venerable Upasena: “Upasena, your followers are inspiring. How do you train them?"

“When anyone asks me for ordination, bhante, I tell them this: ‘I dwell in the forest, I eat almsfood, and I wear rag-robes. If you too will dwell in the forest, eat almsfood, and wear rag-robes, I will ordain you.’ If they agree, I ordain them; otherwise I don’t. Likewise when anyone asks me for dependence. It is in this way, bhante, that I train my followers."

4“Good, good, Upasena, you train your followers well. But do you know the agreement made by the Saṅgha of Sāvatthī?"

“No, bhante."

“The Saṅgha of Sāvatthī has made the following agreement: ‘The Master wishes to be on solitary retreat for three months. No one is to approach the Master except the one who brings him almsfood. Anyone who approaches the Master should be made to confess a pācittiya offence.’"

“Bhante, the Saṅgha of Sāvatthī will become known for this agreement. We, however, do not lay down new rules, nor do we abolish the old ones. We practice and undertake the training rules as they are."

“Good, good, Upasena. One should not lay down new rules, nor should one abolish the old ones. One should practice and undertake the training rules as they are. And, Upasena, I allow those monks who dwell in the forest, who eat almsfood, and who wear rag-robes to come and see me whenever they please."

5At that time many monks were standing outside the gate, thinking: “We will make Venerable Upasena confess a pācittiya offence."

Then Venerable Upasena and his followers rose from their seats, paid homage to the Master, circumambulated him with their right side towards him, and departed. Those monks then said this to Venerable Upasena: “Upasena, do you know the agreement of the Saṅgha of Sāvatthī?"

“The Master asked me the same question, and I replied that I didn’t. He then told me what it was, and I said: ‘Bhante, the Saṅgha of Sāvatthī will become known for this agreement. We, however, do not lay down new rules, nor do we abolish the old ones. We practice and undertake the training rules as they are.’ And the Master has allowed those monks who dwell in the forest, who eat almsfood, and who wear rag-robes to go and see him whenever they please."

6Then those monks thought: “It’s true what Venerable Upasena says."

Soon afterwards the Master, together with many monks, was touring the dwellings, and he saw discarded blankets here and there. He asked the monks: “Whose are these discarded blankets?"

7The monks informed the Master. He then spoke on the Dhamma and addressed the monks: “Well then, monks, I will lay down a training rule for the monks for the following ten reasons: for the well-being of the Saṅgha, for the comfort of the Saṅgha, for the restraint of recalcitrant people, for the ease of well-behaved monks, for the restraint of corruptions that pertain to the present life and for warding off corruptions that pertain to future lives, to give rise to confidence in those without it and to increase the confidence of those who have it, for the continuation of the true Dhamma and for supporting the training. And, monks, this training rule should be recited thus:

Final ruling

8“If a monk is getting a sitting-blanket made, he must incorporate a piece of 25 centimetresThis is according to Bhikkhu Ṭhanissaro’s estimate of the vidatthi, see the Appendix to the Buddhist Monastic Code Volume 1 . According to Monier-Williams’ Sanskrit-English Dictionary (s.v. vitasti), the vidatthi is equivalent to 12 angulis, finger-breadths. from the border of an old blanket in order to make it ugly. If he gets a new sitting-blanket made without incorporating a piece of 25 centimetres from the border of an old blanket, he commits an offence entailing relinquishment and confession."

Definitions

9Sitting (cloth): if it has a border it is so called .

10Blanket: it is made by spreading out, not by weaving.

11Getting made: making or getting made.

12An old blanket: even worn once.

13He must incorporate a piece of 25 centimetres from the border [ … ] in order to make it ugly: to make it strong, he cuts out a circle or a four-sided piece, and he then incorporates it in one place or he spreads it out after pulling it apart.

14Without using a piece of 25 centimetres from the border of an old blanket: in the act of making a new sitting-blanket, or having one made, without incorporating a piece of 25 centimetres from the border of an old blanket, there is an offence of bad conduct. When he gets the sitting-blanket, it becomes subject to relinquishment.

The sitting-blanket is to be relinquished to a Saṅgha, a group, or an individual. “And, monks, it is to be relinquished in this way. … To be expanded as in Nissaggiya Pācittiya 1, verses 13 to 17 with appropriate substitutions. … ‘Bhante, this sitting-blanket which I got made without incorporating a piece of 25 centimetres from the border of an old blanket is to be relinquished. I relinquish it to the Saṅgha.’ … the Saṅgha should give … the Venerables should give … “I give this sitting-blanket back to the Venerable."

Permutations

15If he finishes what he began himself, he commits an offence entailing relinquishment and confession. If others finish what he began himself, he commits an offence entailing relinquishment and confession. If he finishes himself what was begun by others, he commits an offence entailing relinquishment and confession. If others finish what was begun by others, he commits an offence entailing relinquishment and confession.

16If he makes one, or gets one made, for the sake of another, he commits an offence of bad conduct.

Non-offences

17There is no offence: if he makes one incorporating a piece of 25 centimetres from the border of an old blanket; if he does not get such a piece and he makes one incorporating a smaller piece; if he does not get such a smaller piece and he makes one without; if he obtains what was made by another and then uses it; if he makes a canopy, a carpet, a screen-wall, a mattress, or a pillow; if he is insane; if he is the first offender.

18The fifth rule, the training rule on sitting cloths, is finished.

3p_1V_2160Tena kho pana samayena āyasmato upasenassa vangantaputtassa saddhivihāriko bhikkhu bhagavato avidūre nisinno hoti. Atha kho bhagavā taṁ bhikkhuṁ etadavoca: "manāpāni te, bhikkhu, paṁsukūlānī"ti?

"Na kho me, bhante, manāpāni paṁsukūlānī"ti.

"Kissa pana tvaṁ, bhikkhu, paṁsukūliko"ti?

"Upajjhāyo me, bhante, paṁsukūliko. Evaṁ ahampi paṁsukūliko"ti.

Atha kho bhagavā āyasmantaṁ upasenaṁ vangantaputtaṁ etadavoca: "pāsādikā kho tyāyaṁ, upasena, parisā. Kathaṁ tvaṁ, upasena, parisaṁ vinesī"ti?

"Yo maṁ, bhante, upasampadaṁ yācati tamahaṁ evaṁ vadāmi— ‘Ahaṁ kho, āvuso, āraññiko piṇḍapātiko paṁsukūliko. Sace tvampi āraññiko bhavissasi piṇḍapātiko paṁsukūliko, evāhaṁ taṁ upasampādessāmī’ti. Sace me paṭissuṇāti upasampādemi, no ce me paṭissuṇāti na upasampādemi. Yo maṁ nissayaṁ yācati tamahaṁ evaṁ vadāmi— ‘Ahaṁ kho, āvuso, āraññiko piṇḍapātiko paṁsukūliko. Sace tvampi āraññiko bhavissasi piṇḍapātiko paṁsukūliko, evāhaṁ te nissayaṁ dassāmī’ti. Sace me paṭissuṇāti nissayaṁ demi, no ce me paṭissuṇāti na nissayaṁ demi. Evaṁ kho ahaṁ, bhante, parisaṁ vinemī"ti.

4p_1V_2161 "Sādhu sādhu, upasena. Sādhu kho tvaṁ, upasena, parisaṁ vinesi. Jānāsi pana tvaṁ, upasena, sāvatthiyā sanghassa katikan"ti?

"Na kho ahaṁ, bhante, jānāmi sāvatthiyā sanghassa katikan"ti.

"Sāvatthiyā kho, upasena, saṇghena katikā katā— ‘icchatāvuso, bhagavā temāsaṁ paṭisallīyituṁ. Na bhagavā kenaci upasankamitabbo, aññatra ekena piṇḍapātanīhārakena. Yo bhagavantaṁ upasankamati so pācittiyaṁ desāpetabbo’"ti.

"Paññāyissati, bhante, sāvatthiyā sangho sakāya katikāya, na mayaṁ apaññattaṁ paññapessāma paññattaṁ vā na samucchindissāma, yathāpaññattesu sikkhāpadesu samādāya vattissāmā"ti.

"Sādhu sādhu, upasena, apaññattaṁ na paññapetabbaṁ, paññattaṁ vā na samucchinditabbaṁ, yathāpaññattesu sikkhāpadesu samādāya vattitabbaṁ. Anujānāmi, upasena, ye te bhikkhū āraññikā piṇḍapātikā paṁsukūlikā yathāsukhaṁ maṁ dassanāya upasankamantū"ti .
