Bhikkhu Vibhanga

Pārājikas Sanghādisesas Aniyatas Nissaggiya Pācittiyas Pācittiyas Pāṭidesanīyas Sekhiyas Adhikaraṇa-Samathās

Bhikkhu Vibhanga - Sanghādisesa 3: While Overcome By Lust, Using Lewd Speech To A Woman

  • Translated from the Pali by Bhikkhu Brahmali.

Origin story

1At one time the Buddha, the Master, was staying at Sāvatthī in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Park. At that time Venerable Udāyī was staying in the wilderness, in a beautiful dwelling. Just then a number of women came to the monastery in order to see the dwellings. They approached Venerable Udāyī and said:

"Bhante, we would like to see your dwelling."

Then Venerable Udāyī, after showing his dwelling to those women, praised and disparaged their private parts, he requested and implored, asked and enquired, described, instructed and abused. Those women among them who were wanton and shameless flirted with Venerable Udāyī, they called out to him, laughed with him and made fun of him. But those women among them who had a sense of shame, after leaving, complained to the monks: "Bhante, this is not suitable and appropriate. We would not want to hear this from our own husbands, let alone from Venerable Udāyī."

"How can Venerable Udāyī speak lewdly to women?"

2They rebuked Venerable Udāyī in many ways and informed the Master. And the Master convened the Saṅgha and questioned Venerable Udāyī:

"Is it true, Udāyī, that you spoke lewdly to women?"

"Itʼs true, Master."

The Buddha, the Master, rebuked him: "Itʼs not suitable, foolish man, itʼs not becoming, itʼs not proper, itʼs not worthy of a recluse, itʼs not allowable, it should not to be done. How could you speak lewdly to women? Have I not taught the Dhamma in many ways for the sake of dispassion, not for the sake of passion … for the stilling of the fevers of sense desire? This will not give rise to confidence in those without it … And, monks, this training rule should be recited thus:

Final ruling

3"If a monk, lustful and with a distorted mind, speaks lewdIn his study A Translation and Analysis of the Pātimokkha Bhikkhu Ñāṇatusita makes the point that duṭṭhulla in its own right does not seem to mean 'lewd,' but rather 'coarse' or 'gross'. However, in the present context the words spoken are specifically said to be "connected with sexual intercourse", and thus 'lewd' seems appropriate. words connected with sexual intercourse to a woman, as a young man might to a young woman, he commits an offence entailing suspension."

Definitions

4A: whoever … Monk: … The monk ordained by a complete Saṅgha through a procedure of one motion and three announcements, which is unchallengeable and fit to stand—this sort of monk is meant in this case.

5Lustful: having lust, longing for, in love with.

6With a distorted mind: a mind with desire is distorted, a mind with anger is distorted, a confused mind is distorted. But in this case distorted refers to a mind with desire.

7Woman: a human female, not a female spirit, not a female ghost, not a female animal. She understands and is capable of discerning bad speech and good speech, what is lewd and what is not lewd.

8Lewd words: speech connected with the anus, the vagina, or sexual intercourse.

9Speaks: misconduct is being referred to.

10As a young man might to a young woman: a male youth to a female youth, a boy to a girl, a male enjoying sense pleasures to a female enjoying sense pleasures.

11Connected with sexual intercourse: connected with the sexual act.

12He commits an offence entailing suspension: … This is the name and designation of this class of offence. Therefore, too, it is said that he commits an offence entailing suspension.

Permutations

Summary

13Referring to the two private orifices, one praises, disparages, requests, implores, asks, enquires, describes, instructs, abuses.

Sub-definitions

14Praises: extols, praises, commends the two private orifices.

15Disparages: despises, reviles, censures the two private orifices.

16Requests: saying, "Give to me; you ought to give to me."

17Implores: saying, "When will you please your mother? When will you please your father? When will you please the gods? When will there be a good opportunity, a good time, a good moment? When will I have sexual intercourse with you?"

18Asks: saying, "How do you give to your husband? How do you give to your lover?"

19Enquires: saying, "So in this way you give to your husband; in this way you give to your lover?"

20Describes: having been asked, one says, "Give in this way. When you do, you will be dear and pleasing to your husband."

21Instructs: not having been asked, one says, "Give in this way. When you do, you will be dear and pleasing to your husband."

22Abuses: saying, "You are lacking sexual characteristics, you are defective in sexual characteristics, you donʼt menstruate, you menstruate continuously, you use the menstruation cloth continuously, youʼre dripping, youʼre deformed, youʼre a female paṇḍaka, you have male characteristics, your sexual characteristics are mixed, youʼre a hermaphrodite.

Exposition

23It is a woman, he perceives her as a woman, and he has lust. If the monk, referring to the two private orifices of the woman, praises, disparages, requests, implores, asks, enquires, describes, instructs, abuses, he commits an offence entailing suspension. … To be expanded as in Suspension 2, MS paragraphs 1022–1034. …

24It is two women, he perceives them both as women, and he has lust. If the monk, referring to the two private orifices of both women, praises, disparages … abuses, he commits two offences entailing suspension. … To be expanded as in Suspension 2, MS paragraphs 1037–1045. …

25It is a woman and a paṇḍaka, but he perceives both as women, and he has lust. If the monk, referring to the two private orifices of both of them,Although a paṇḍaka probably only has one such orifice, the monk thinks he has two, since he mistakes him for a woman. praises, disparages … abuses, he commits one offence entailing suspension and one offence of bad conduct. … To be expanded as in Suspension 2, MS paragraphs 1046–1060. …

26… abuses, he commits a grave offence. …

27It is two women, he perceives them both as women, and he has lust. If the monk, apart from the two private orifices, referring to any part of both women below the collar bone but above the knees, praises, disparages … abuses, he commits two grave offences. …

28It is a woman and a paṇḍaka, but he perceives both as women, and he has lust. If the monk, apart from the two private orifices, referring to any part of both of them below the collar bone but above the knees, praises, disparages … abuses, he commits one grave offence and one offence of bad conduct. …

29It is a woman, he perceives her as a woman, and he has lust. If the monk, referring to any part of the woman above the collar bone or below the knees, praises, disparages … abuses, he commits an offence of bad conduct. …

30It is two women, he perceives them both as women, and he has lust. If the monk, referring to any part of both women above the colar bone or below the knees, praises, disparages … abuses, he commits two offences of bad conduct. …

31It is a woman and a paṇḍaka, but he perceives both as women, and he has lust. If the monk, referring to any part of both of them above the collar bone or below the knees, praises, disparages … abuses, he commits two offences of bad conduct. …

32It is a woman, he perceives her as a woman, and he has lust. If the monk, referring to anything connected to the womanʼs body, praises, disparages … abuses, he commits an offence of bad conduct. …

33It is two women, he perceives them both as women, and he has lust. If the monk, referring to anything connected to the body of both women, praises, disparages … abuses, he commits two offences of bad conduct. …

34It is a woman and a paṇḍaka, but he perceives both as women, and he has lust. If the monk, referring to anything connected to the body of both of them, praises, disparages … abuses, he commits two offences of bad conduct. …

Non-offences

35There is no offence: if he is aiming at what is beneficial; if he is aiming at teaching the Dhamma; if he is aiming at giving instruction; if he is insane; if he is the first offender.

Case rulings

Mnemonic list

36Red, coarse, thick,
Rough, long, sown;
I hope the path has an end,
Faith, with a gift, with work.

Case details

37At one time a certain woman was wearing a newly dyed shawl. A certain monk, being lustful, said to her: "Sister, is that red thing yours?"

She did not understand and said: "Yes, Venerable, itʼs a newly dyed shawl."

He became anxious and thought, "The Master has laid down a training rule. Could it be that I have committed an offence entailing suspension?" He informed the Master.

"Monk, there is no offence entailing suspension, but there is an offence of bad conduct."

38At one time a certain woman was wearing a rough shawl … "Sister, is that coarse hair yours?"

She did not understand and said: "Yes, Venerable, itʼs a rough shawl." …

"Monk, there is no offence entailing suspension, but there is an offence of bad conduct."

39At one time a certain woman was wearing a newly woven shawl … "Sister, is that thick hair yours?"

She did not understand and said: "Yes, Venerable, itʼs a newly woven shawl." …

"Monk, there is no offence entailing suspension, but there is an offence of bad conduct."

40At one time a certain woman was wearing a rough shawl … "Sister, is that rough hair yours?" …

"Yes, Venerable, itʼs a rough shawl." …

"Monk, there is no offence entailing suspension, but there is an offence of bad conduct."

41"Sister, is that long hair yours?" …

""Monk, there is no offence entailing suspension, but there is an offence of bad conduct."

42At one time a certain woman who had just had a field sown was coming along. A certain monk, being lustful, said to her: "Have you sown, sister?"

She did not understand and said: "Yes, Venerable, but the furrow is not yet closed."

He became anxious … "Monk, there is no offence entailing suspension, but there is an offence of bad conduct."

43At one time a certain monk saw a female wanderer coming in the opposite direction, and being lustful he said to her: "Sister, I hope the path has an end?"

She did not understand and said: "Yes, monk, just keep going."

He became anxious … "Monk, there is no offence entailing suspension, but there is a serious offence."

44At one time a certain monk, being lustful, said to a certain woman: "You have confidence, sister, but you donʼt give us what you give your husband."

"What is that, bhante?"

"Sexual intercourse."

He became anxious … "Monk, There is an offence entailing suspension."

45At one time a certain monk, being lustful, said to a certain woman: "You have confidence, sister, yet you donʼt give us the highest gift."

"What is the highest gift, bhante?"

"Sexual intercourse."

He became anxious … "Monk, There is an offence entailing suspension."

46At one time a certain woman was doing some work. A certain monk, being lustful, said to her: "Stand, sister, I will work." … "Sit, sister, I will work." … "Lie down, sister, I will work."

She did not understand.

He became anxious … "Monk, there is no offence entailing suspension, but there is an offence of bad conduct."

47The third training rule on lewd speech is finished.
