Bhikkhuni Vibhanga

Pārājikas Sanghādisesas Nissaggiya Pācittiyas Pācittiyas Pāṭidesanīyas Sekhiyas Adhikaraṇa-Samathās

Bhikkhuni Vibhanga - Pācittiya 109:Bhikkhu's Pc 13 Not to Blame or Slander a Bhikkhuni.

  • Translated from the Pali by Brahmali Bhikkhu.

Origin story

First sub-story

1At one time the Buddha, the Master, was staying at Rājagaha in the squirrel sanctuary in the Bamboo Grove. At that time Venerable Dabba the Mallian assigned the lodgings and designated the meals. Just then some monks who were followers of Mettiya and Bhummaja were newly ordained. They had little merit, and they obtained inferior lodgings and meals. They then made complaints about Venerable Dabba to other monks: “Dabba the Mallian assigns lodgings and designates meals based on favouritism."

2The monks of few desires … complained and criticised them: “How can those monks who are followers of Mettiya and Bhummaja make complaints about Venerable Dabba to other monks?" … “Is it true, monks, that you are doing this?" “It’s true, Master." The Buddha, the Master, criticised them: “ … Foolish men, how can you make complaints about Dabba to other monks? This will not give rise to confidence in those without it … And, monks, this training rule should be recited thus:

Preliminary ruling

3“If a monk makes a complaint, he commits an offence entailing confession."

4In this way the Master laid down this training rule for the monks.

Second sub-story

5Knowing that the Master had prohibited making complaints, those monks who were followers of Mettiya and Bhummaja thought of other ways of getting monks to hear about it. They then criticised Venerable Dabba the Mallian when other monks were nearby: “Dabba assigns lodgings and designates meals based on favouritism."

The monks of few desires … complained and criticised them: “How can those monks who are followers of Mettiya and Bhummaja criticise Venerable Dabba?" … “Is it true, monks, that you are doing this?" “It’s true, Master." The Buddha, the Master, criticised them: “ … Foolish men, how can you criticise Dabba? This will not give rise to confidence in those without it … And so, monks, this training rule should be recited thus:

Final ruling

6“If a monk makes a complaint or criticises, he commits an offence entailing confession."

Definitions

7Makes a complaint: when someone who is fully ordained is the assigner of lodgings or the designator of meals or the distributor of rice-porridge or the distributor of fruit or the distributor of cakes or the distributor of small requisites, and he has been agreed upon by the Saṅgha as such, then if a monk makes a complaint about him, or criticises him, to one who is fully ordained, desiring to disparage him, desiring to give him a bad reputation, desiring to humiliate him, he commits an offence entailing confession.

Permutations

8If it is a legitimate act of the Saṅgha, and he perceives it as legitimate, and he makes a complaint or criticises, he commits an offence entailing confession. If it is a legitimate act of the Saṅgha, but he has doubts about it, and he makes a complaint or criticises, he commits an offence entailing confession. If it is a legitimate act of the Saṅgha, but he perceives it as illegitimate, and he makes a complaint or criticises, he commits an offence entailing confession.

If he makes a complaint about him, or criticises him, to one who is not fully ordained, he commits an offence of bad conduct.

When someone who is fully ordained is the assigner of lodgings or the designator of meals or the distributor of rice-porridge or the distributor of fruit or the distributor of cakes or the distributor of small requisites, but he has not been agreed upon by the Saṅgha as such, then if a monk makes a complaint about him, or criticises him, to one who is fully ordained or to one who is not fully ordained, desiring to disparage him, desiring to give him a bad reputation, desiring to humiliate him, he commits an offence of bad conduct.

When someone who is not fully ordained is the assigner of lodgings or the designator of meals or the distributor of rice-porridge or the distributor of fruit or the distributor of cakes or the distributor of small requisites, whether he has been agreed upon by the Saṅgha as such or not, then if a monk makes a complaint about him, or criticises him, to one who is fully ordained or to one who is not fully ordained, desiring to disparage him, desiring to give him a bad reputation, desiring to humiliate him, he commits an offence of bad conduct.

If it is an illegitimate act of the Saṅgha, but he perceives it as legitimate, he commits an offence of bad conduct.

If it is an illegitimate act of the Saṅgha, but he has doubts about it, he commits an offence of bad conduct.

If it is an illegitimate act of the Saṅgha, and he perceives it as illegitimate, he commits an offence of bad conduct.

Non-offences

9There is no offence: if he makes a complaint about, or criticises, one who normally acts from desire, anger, confusion, fear; if he is insane; if he is the first offender.

10The third rule, the training rule on making complaints, is finished.
