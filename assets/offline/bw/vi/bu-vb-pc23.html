Bhikkhu Vibhanga - Pācittiya 23: The Training Rule on Nuns’ Dwelling Places.

  • Translated from the Pali by Bhikkhu Brahmali.   (More copyright information)

Origin story

First sub-story

1At one time the Buddha, the Master, was staying in the Sakyan country at Kapilavatthu in the Banyan Tree Monastery. At that time monks from the group of six went to the nuns’ dwelling place and instructed the nuns belonging to the group of six.

Soon afterwards, the nuns said to those nuns from the group of six: “Come, venerables, let’s go to the instruction."

“There’s no need; the venerables from the group of six came and instructed us right here."

The nuns complained and criticised those monks from the group of six: “How can those monks from the group of six go and instruct the nuns at their dwelling place?" And those nuns informed the monks.

2The monks of few desires … complained and criticised those monks: “How can those monks from the group of six go and instruct the nuns at their dwelling place?" … “Is it true, monks, that you do this?" “It’s true, Master." The Buddha, the Master, criticised them: “ … Foolish men, how can you go and instruct the nuns at their dwelling place? This will not give rise to confidence in those without it … And, monks, this training rule should be recited thus:

Preliminary ruling

3“If a monk goes to a nun’s dwelling place, and then instructs her, he commits an offence entailing confession."

4In this way the Master laid down this training rule for the monks.

Second sub-story

5Soon afterwards Mahāpajāpati Gotamī became sick. Some senior monks went to see her and said: “We hope you are bearing up, Gotamī, we hope you are getting better?"

“I am not bearing up, venerables, and I am not getting better. Please teach me the Dhamma."

“It’s not allowable for us to go and teach the Dhamma to a nun at her dwelling place." And being afraid of wrongdoing, they did not teach her.

Then, after dressing in the morning, the Master took his bowl and robe and went to Mahāpajāpati Gotamī, where he sat down on a prepared seat. He then said to her: “I hope you are bearing up, Gotamī, I hope you are getting better?"

“Previously, bhante, senior monks would come and teach me the Dhamma, and because of that I would be at ease. But now they do not teach because they are afraid of wrongdoing, saying, ‘It’s been prohibited by the Master.’ And because of that I am not at ease."

Then, after instructing, inspiring, and gladdening her with a Dhamma talk, the Master rose from his seat and departed. Soon afterwards the Master spoke on the Dhamma and addressed the monks: “Monks, I allow you to go and instruct a sick nun at her dwelling place. And so, monks, this training rule should be recited thus:

Final ruling

6“If a monk goes to a nun’s dwelling place and then instructs her, unless there is an appropriate reason, he commits an offence entailing confession. The appropriate reason is that the nun is sick."

Definitions

7A: whoever … Monk: … The monk who is ordained by a complete Saṅgha through a procedure of one motion and three announcements, which is unchallengeable and fit to stand; this sort of monk is meant in this case.

8A nun’s dwelling place: where nuns stay for even a single night.

9Goes to: goes there.

10Nuns: fully ordained by both Sanghas.

11Instructs: if he instructs about the eight important rules, he commits an offence entailing confession.

12Unless there is an appropriate reason: except at an appropriate time.

13Sick: the nun is not able to go to the instruction or to a meeting of the Saṅgha.

Permutations

14If she is fully ordained, and he perceives her as fully ordained, and he goes to her dwelling place and then instructs her, unless there is is an appropriate reason, he commits an offence entailing confession. If she is fully ordained, but he has doubts about it, and he goes to her dwelling place and then instructs her, unless there is an appropriate reason, he commits an offence entailing confession. If she is fully ordained, but he perceives her as not fully ordained, and he goes to her dwelling place and then instructs her, unless there is an appropriate reason, he commits an offence entailing confession.

15If he instructs her with another teaching, he commits an offence of bad conduct. If he instructs a nun ordained only on one side, he commits an offence of bad conduct. If she is not fully ordained, but he perceives her as fully ordained, he commits an offence of bad conduct. If she is not fully ordained, but he has doubts about it, he commits an offence of bad conduct. If she is not fully ordained, and he perceives her as not fully ordained, there is no offence.

Non-offences

16There is no offence: if there is an appropriate reason; if he gives a summary; if he conducts an examination; if he recites when asked to do so; if he asks a question; if he replies when asked a question; if he is speaking for the benefit of someone else and the nuns listen in; if it is to a trainee nun; if it is to a novice nun; if he is insane; if he is the first offender.

17The third rule, the training rule on nuns’ dwelling places, is finished.

5p_2V_535, msdiv159Tena kho pana samayena mahāpajāpati gotamī gilānā hoti. Therā bhikkhū yena mahāpajāpati gotamī tenupasankamiṁsu; upasankamitvā mahāpajāpatiṁ gotamiṁ etadavocuṁ: "Kacci, gotami, khamanīyaṁ, kacci yāpanīyan"ti?

"Na me, ayyā, khamanīyaṁ na yāpanīyaṁ". "Inghayyā, dhammaṁ desethā"ti.

"Na, bhagini, kappati bhikkhunupassayaṁ upasankamitvā bhikkhuniyo dhammaṁ desetun"ti kukkuccāyantā na desesuṁ.

Atha kho bhagavā pubbaṇhasamayaṁ nivāsetvā pattacīvaramādāya yena mahāpajāpati gotamī tenupasankami; upasankamitvā paññatte āsane nisīdi. Nisajja kho bhagavā mahāpajāpatiṁ gotamiṁ etadavoca: "Kacci, gotami, khamanīyaṁ, kacci yāpanīyan"ti?

"Pubbe me, bhante, therā bhikkhū āgantvā dhammaṁ desenti. Tena me phāsu hoti. Idāni pana— ‘bhagavatā paṭikkhittan’ti, kukkuccāyantā na desenti. Tena me na phāsu hotī"ti. Atha kho bhagavā mahāpajāpatiṁ gotamiṁ dhammiyā kathāya sandassetvā samādapetvā samuttejetvā sampahaṁsetvā uṭṭhāyāsanā pakkāmi.
