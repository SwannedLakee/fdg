Bhikkhu Vibhanga

Pārājikas Sanghādisesas Aniyatas Nissaggiya Pācittiyas Pācittiyas Pāṭidesanīyas Sekhiyas Adhikaraṇa-Samathās

Bhikkhu Vibhanga - Pācittiya 1: The Training Rule on Lying.

  • Translated from the Pali by Bhikkhu Brahmali.   (More copyright information)

1Venerables, these ninety-two rules entailing confession come up for recitation.

2Homage to the Master, the Arahant, the fully Awakened One

Origin story

3Bu-Pc.1.1At one time the Buddha, the Master, was staying at Sāvatthī in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. At that time Hatthaka the Sakyan was beaten in debate. While talking with members of other sects, he would assert something after denying it, and he would deny something after asserting it. He evaded the issues, deliberately lied, and after reaching an agreement he would speak deceptively. The members of other sects complained and criticised him: “How can Hatthaka, while talking with us, assert things after denying them, deny things after asserting them, evade the issues, deliberately lie, and after reaching an agreement speak deceptively?"

4Monks heard the complaints of those members of other sects, and they went to Hatthaka and said: “Is it true, Hatthaka, that while talking with members of other sects, you assert things after denying them, deny things after asserting them, evade the issues, deliberately lie, and after reaching an agreement speak deceptively?"

“These members of other sects should be beaten by any means; they should not be allowed to win."

The monks of few desires … complained and criticised him: “How can Hatthaka, while talking with members of other sects, assert things after denying them, deny things after asserting them, evade the issues, deliberately lie, and after reaching an agreement speak deceptively?"

5After criticising Hatthaka in many ways, they informed the Master. The Master then convened the Saṅgha and questioned Hatthaka: “Is it true, Hatthaka, that while talking with members of other sects, you assert things after denying them, deny things after asserting them, evade the issues, deliberately lie, and after reaching an agreement speak deceptively?"

“It’s true, Master."

The Buddha, the Master, criticised him: “ … Foolish man, how can you act in this way? This will not give rise to confidence in those without it … And, monks, this training rule should be recited thus:

Final ruling

6“If a monk deliberately lies, he commits an offence entailing confession."

Definitions

7Deliberately lies: the speech of one who is intent on deceiving—his words, his way of speaking, his breaking into speech, his verbal expression, his eight kinds of ignoble speech: he says that he has seen what he has not seen; he says that he has heard what he has not heard; he says that he has sensed what he has not sensed; he says that he has cognised what he has not cognised; he says that he has not seen what he has seen; he says that he has not heard what he has heard; he says that he has not sensed what he has sensed; he says that he has not cognised what he has cognised.

Permutations

Definitions

8Not seen: not seen with the eye. Not heard: not heard with the ear. Not sensed: not smelled with the nose, not tasted with the tongue, not touched with the body. Not cognised: not cognised with the mind. Seen: seen with the eye. Heard: heard with the ear. Sensed: smelled with the nose, tasted with the tongue, touched with the body. Cognised: cognised with the mind.

Exposition

Falsely claiming to have experienced what he has not experienced: a single sense door

9If he deliberately lies, saying that he has seen what he has not seen, he commits an offence entailing confession when three conditions are fulfilled: before he has lied, he knows he is going to lie; while lying, he knows he is lying; after he has lied, he knows he has lied.

10If he deliberately lies, saying that he has seen what he has not seen, he commits an offence entailing confession when four conditions are fulfilled: before he has lied, he knows he is going to lie; while lying, he knows he is lying; after he has lied, he knows he has lied; in misrepresenting his view (of what is true).

11If he deliberately lies, saying that he has seen what he has not seen, he commits an offence entailing confession when five conditions are fulfilled: before he has lied, he knows he is going to lie; while lying, he knows he is lying; after he has lied, he knows he has lied; in misrepresenting his view (of what is true); in misrepresenting his belief (of what is true).

12If he deliberately lies, saying that he has seen what he has not seen, he commits an offence entailing confession when six conditions are fulfilled: before he has lied, he knows he is going to lie; while lying, he knows he is lying; after he has lied, he knows he has lied; in misrepresenting his view (of what is true); in misrepresenting his belief (of what is true); in misrepresenting his acceptance (of what is true).

13If he deliberately lies, saying that he has seen what he has not seen, he commits an offence entailing confession when seven conditions are fulfilled: before he has lied, he knows he is going to lie; while lying, he knows he is lying; after he has lied, he knows he has lied; in misrepresenting his view (of what is true); in misrepresenting his belief (of what is true); in misrepresenting his acceptance (of what is true); in misrepresenting his sentiment (of what is true).

14If he deliberately lies, saying that he says that he has heard what he has not heard … saying that he has sensed what he has not sensed … saying that he has cognised what he has not cognised, he commits an offence entailing confession when three conditions are fulfilled: before he has lied, he knows he is going to lie; while lying, he knows he is lying; after he has lied, he knows he has lied.

15… when four conditions are fulfilled … when five conditions are fulfilled … when six conditions are fulfilled … If he deliberately lies, saying that he has cognised what he has not cognised, he commits an offence entailing confession when seven conditions are fulfilled: before he has lied, he knows he is going to lie; while lying, he knows he is lying; after he has lied, he knows he has lied; in misrepresenting his view (of what is true); in misrepresenting his belief (of what is true); in misrepresenting his acceptance (of what is true); in misrepresenting his sentiment (of what is true).

Falsely claiming to have experienced what he has not experienced: multiple sense doors

16If he deliberately lies, saying that he has seen and heard what he has not seen, he commits an offence entailing confession when three conditions are fulfilled … If he deliberately lies, saying that he has seen and sensed what he has not seen, he commits an offence entailing confession when three conditions are fulfilled … If he deliberately lies, saying that he has seen and cognised what he has not seen, he commits an offence entailing confession when three conditions are fulfilled … If he deliberately lies, saying that he has seen and heard and sensed what he has not seen, he commits an offence entailing confession when three conditions are fulfilled … If he deliberately lies, saying that he has seen and heard and cognised what he has not seen, he commits an offence entailing confession when three conditions are fulfilled … If he deliberately lies, saying that he has seen and heard and sensed and cognised what he has not seen, he commits an offence entailing confession when three conditions are fulfilled: …

17If he deliberately lies, saying that he has heard and sensed what he has not heard, he commits an offence entailing confession when three conditions are fulfilled … If he deliberately lies, saying that he has heard and cognised what he has not heard, he commits an offence entailing confession when three conditions are fulfilled … If he deliberately lies, saying that he has heard and seen what he has not heard, he commits an offence entailing confession when three conditions are fulfilled … If he dhrefeliberately lies, saying that he has heard and sensed and cognised what he has not heard, he commits an offence entailing confession when three conditions are fulfilled … If he deliberately lies, saying that he has heard and sensed and seen what he has not heard, he commits an offence entailing confession when three conditions are fulfilled … If he deliberately lies, saying that he has heard and sensed and cognised and seen what he has not heard, he commits an offence entailing confession when three conditions are fulfilled: …

18If he deliberately lies, saying that he has sensed and cognised what he has not sensed, he commits an offence entailing confession when three conditions are fulfilled … If he deliberately lies, saying that he has sensed and seen what he has not sensed, he commits an offence entailing confession when three conditions are fulfilled … If he deliberately lies, saying that he has sensed and heard what he has not sensed, he commits an offence entailing confession when three conditions are fulfilled … If he deliberately lies, saying that he has sensed and cognised and seen what he has not sensed, he commits an offence entailing confession when three conditions are fulfilled … If he deliberately lies, saying that he has sensed and cognised and heard what he has not sensed, he commits an offence entailing confession when three conditions are fulfilled … If he deliberately lies, saying that he has sensed and cognised and seen and heard what he has not sensed, he commits an offence entailing confession when three conditions are fulfilled: …

19If he deliberately lies, saying that he has cognised and seen what he has not cognised, he commits an offence entailing confession when three conditions are fulfilled … If he deliberately lies, saying that he has cognised and heard what he has not cognised, he commits an offence entailing confession when three conditions are fulfilled … If he deliberately lies, saying that he has cognised and sensed what he has not cognised, he commits an offence entailing confession when three conditions are fulfilled … If he deliberately lies, saying that he has cognised and seen and heard what he has not cognised, he commits an offence entailing confession when three conditions are fulfilled … If he deliberately lies, saying that he has cognised and seen and sensed what he has not cognised, he commits an offence entailing confession when three conditions are fulfilled … If he deliberately lies, saying that he has cognised and seen and heard and sensed what he has not cognised, he commits an offence entailing confession when three conditions are fulfilled: …

Falsely claiming not to have experienced what he has experienced

20If he deliberately lies, saying that he has not seen what he has seen, he commits an offence entailing confession when three conditions are fulfilled … saying that he has not heard what he has heard … saying that he has not sensed what he has sensed … saying that he has not cognised what he has cognised, he commits an offence entailing confession when three conditions are fulfilled …

Falsely claiming to have experienced with one sense what he has experienced with another

21If he lies in full awareness, saying that he has heard what he has seen, he commits an offense entailing confession when three conditions are fulfilled … saying that he has sensed what he has seen … saying that he has known what he has seen, he commits an offense entailing confession when three conditions are fulfilled … If he lies in full awareness, saying that he has heard and sensed what he has seen, he commits an offense entailing confession when three conditions are fulfilled … saying that he has heard and known what he has seen … saying that he has heard and sensed and known what he seen, he commits an offense entailing confession when three conditions are fulfilled …

22If he deliberately lies, saying that he has sensed what he has heard, he commits an offence entailing confession when three conditions are fulfilled … saying that he has cognised what he has heard … saying that he has seen what he has heard, he commits an offence entailing confession when three conditions are fulfilled … If he deliberately lies, saying that he has sensed and cognised what he has heard, he commits an offence entailing confession when three conditions are fulfilled … saying that he has sensed and seen what he has heard … saying that he has sensed and cognised and seen what he heard, he commits an offence entailing confession when three conditions are fulfilled …

23If he deliberately lies, saying that he has cognised what he has sensed, he commits an offence entailing confession when three conditions are fulfilled … saying that he has seen what he has sensed … saying that he has heard whathref he has sensed, he commits an offence entailing confession when three conditions are fulfilled … If he deliberately lies, saying that he has cognised and seen what he has sensed, he commits an offence entailing confession when three conditions are fulfilled … saying that he has cognised and heard and sensed what he has heard … saying that he has cognised and seen and heard what he sensed, he commits an offence entailing confession when three conditions are fulfilled …

24If he deliberately lies, saying that he has seen what he has cognised, he commits an offence entailing confession when three conditions are fulfilled … saying that he has heard what he has cognised … saying that he has sensed what he has cognised, he commits an offence entailing confession when three conditions are fulfilled … If he deliberately lies, saying that he has seen and heard what he has cognised, he commits an offence entailing confession when three conditions are fulfilled … saying that he has seen and sensed what he has cognised … saying that he has seen and heard and sensed what he cognised, he commits an offence entailing confession when three conditions are fulfilled …

Making claims while having doubts

25If he has doubts about what he has seen, is not sure about what he has seen, does not remember what he has seen, is confused about what he has seen … If he has doubts about what he has heard, is not sure about what he has heard, does not remember what he has heard, is confused about what he has heard … If he has doubts about what he has sensed, is not sure about what he has sensed, does not remember what he has sensed, is confused about what he has sensed … If he has doubts about what he has cognised, is not sure about what he has cognised, does not remember what he has cognised, is confused about what he has cognised, but he deliberately lies, saying that he has cognised and seen … is confused about what he has cognised, but he deliberately lies, saying that he has cognised and heard … is confused about what he has cognised, but he deliberately lies, saying that he has cognised and sensed … is confused about what he has cognised, but he deliberately lies, saying that he has cognised and seen and heard … is confused about what he has cognised, but he deliberately lies, saying that he has cognised and seen and sensed … is confused about what he has cognised, but he deliberately lies, saying that he has cognised and seen and heard and sensed, he commits an offence entailing confession when three conditions are fulfilled … This series seems to be missing one combination: “ … is confused about what he has cognised, but he deliberately lies, saying that he has cognised and heard and sensed … "

26… when four conditions are fulfilled … when five conditions are fulfilled … when six conditions are fulfilled … is confused about what he has cognised, but he deliberately lies, saying that he has cognised and seen and heard and sensed, he commits an offence entailing confession when seven conditions are fulfilled: before he has lied, he knows he is going to lie; while lying, he knows he is lying; after he has lied, he knows he has lied; in misrepresenting his view (of what is true); in misrepresenting his belief (of what is true); in misrepresenting his acceptance (of what is true); in misrepresenting his sentiment (of what is true).

Non-offences

27There is no offence: if he speaks playfully; if he is speaks too fast (speaks playfully means: speaking hastily; speaks too fast means: thinking to say one thing, he says something else); if he is insane; if he is the first offender.

28The first rule, the training rule on lying, is finished.

21p_2V_21, msdiv8Tīhākārehi diṭṭhaṁ "sutaṁ me"ti … pe … tīhākārehi diṭṭhaṁ "mutaṁ me"ti … pe … tīhākārehi diṭṭhaṁ "viññātaṁ me"ti sampajānamusā bhaṇantassa āpatti pācittiyassa … pe … tīhākārehi diṭṭhaṁ "sutañca me mutañcā"ti … pe … tīhākārehi diṭṭhaṁ "sutañca me viññātañcā"ti … pe … tīhākārehi diṭṭhaṁ "sutañca me mutañca viññātañcā"ti sampajānamusā bhaṇantassa āpatti pācittiyassa … pe … .

22p_2V_22Tīhākārehi sutaṁ "mutaṁ me"ti … pe … tīhākārehi sutaṁ "viññātaṁ me"ti … pe … tīhākārehi sutaṁ "diṭṭhaṁ me"ti sampajānamusā bhaṇantassa āpatti pācittiyassa … pe … tīhākārehi sutaṁ "mutañca me viññātañcā"ti … pe … tīhākārehi sutaṁ "mutañca me diṭṭhañcā"ti … pe … tīhākārehi sutaṁ "mutañca me viññātañca diṭṭhañcā"ti sampajānamusā bhaṇantassa āpatti pācittiyassa … pe … .

23p_2V_23Tīhākārehi mutaṁ "viññātaṁ me"ti … pe … tīhākārehi mutaṁ "diṭṭhaṁ me"ti … pe … tīhākārehi mutaṁ "sutaṁ me"ti sampajānamusā bhaṇantassa āpatti pācittiyassa … pe … tīhākārehi mutaṁ "viññātañca me diṭṭhañcā"ti … pe … tīhākārehi mutaṁ "viññātañca me sutañcā"ti … pe … tīhākārehi mutaṁ "viññātañca me diṭṭhañca sutañcā"ti sampajānamusā bhaṇantassa āpatti pācittiyassa … pe … .

24p_2V_24Tīhākārehi viññātaṁ "diṭṭhaṁ me"ti … pe … tīhākārehi viññātaṁ "sutaṁ me"ti … pe … tīhākārehi viññātaṁ "mutaṁ me"ti sampajānamusā bhaṇantassa āpatti pācittiyassa … pe … tīhākārehi viññātaṁ "diṭṭhañca me sutañcā"ti … pe … tīhākārehi viññātaṁ "diṭṭhañca me mutañcā"ti … pe … tīhākārehi viññātaṁ "diṭṭhañca me sutañca mutañcā"ti sampajānamusā bhaṇantassa āpatti pācittiyassa … pe … .

 

25p_2V_25, msdiv9Tīhākārehi diṭṭhe vematiko diṭṭhaṁ nokappeti, diṭṭhaṁ nassarati, diṭṭhaṁ pamuṭṭho hoti … pe … sute vematiko sutaṁ nokappeti, sutaṁ nassarati, sutaṁ pamuṭṭho hoti … pe … mute vematiko mutaṁ nokappeti, mutaṁ nassarati, mutaṁ pamuṭṭho hoti … pe … viññāte vematiko viññātaṁ nokappeti, viññātaṁ nassarati, viññātaṁ pamuṭṭho hoti … viññātañca me diṭṭhañcāti … pe … viññātaṁ pamuṭṭho hoti … viññātañca me sutañcāti … pe … viññātaṁ pamuṭṭho hoti; viññātañca me mutañcāti … pe … viññātaṁ pamuṭṭho hoti; viññātañca me diṭṭhañca sutañcāti … pe … viññātaṁ pamuṭṭho hoti; viññātañca me diṭṭhañca mutañcāti … pe … viññātaṁ pamuṭṭho hoti; viññātañca me diṭṭhañca sutañca mutañcāti sampajānamusā bhaṇantassa āpatti pācittiyassa.

26p_2V_26, msdiv10Catūhākārehi … pe … pañcahākārehi … pe … chahākārehi … pe … sattahākārehi … pe … viññātaṁ pamuṭṭho hoti, viññātañca me diṭṭhañca sutañca mutañcāti sampajānamusā bhaṇantassa āpatti pācittiyassa— pubbevassa hoti "musā bhaṇissan"ti, bhaṇantassa hoti "musā bhaṇāmī"ti, bhaṇitassa hoti "musā mayā bhaṇitan"ti, vinidhāya diṭṭhiṁ, vinidhāya khantiṁ, vinidhāya ruciṁ, vinidhāya bhāvaṁ.

 

27p_2V_27, msdiv11Anāpatti— davā bhaṇati, ravā bhaṇati. "Davā bhaṇati nāma sahasā bhaṇati. Ravā bhaṇati nāma ‘aññaṁ bhaṇissāmī’ti aññaṁ bhaṇati". Ummattakassa, ādikammikassāti.
