Vinaya Pitaka - Monastic Rules

Bhikkhu Vibhanga - Pācittiya 35: The Training Rule on Satisfaction.

  • Translated from the Pali by Bhikkhu Brahmali.   (More copyright information)

Origin story

First sub-story

1At one time the Buddha, the Master, was staying at Sāvatthī in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. At that time a certain brahman had invited monks for a meal. When the monks had eaten and expressed their satisfaction, they went to their respective families, where some ate and others got almsfood and left.

Soon afterwards that brahman said this to his neighbours: “The monks have been satisfied by me. Come, and I will satisfy you too."

“How could you satisfy us too? Those monks who were invited by you came to our houses, where some ate and others got almsfood and left."

2Then that brahman complained and criticised those monks: “How can the venerables eat in our house and afterwards eat elsewhere? Am I not able to give them as much as they want?"

3Monks heard the complaints of that brahman, finish their meal and express their satisfaction, and then eat elsewhere? This will not give rise to confidence in those without it … And, monks, this training rule should be recited thus:

Preliminary ruling

4“If a monk who has eaten and expressed his satisfaction eats staple or non-staple food, he commits an offence entailing confession."

5In this way the Master laid down this training rule for the monks.

Second sub-story

6Soon afterwards monks were bringing back fine almsfood for the sick monks. But because they were not able to eat as much as they had intended, the monks threw the leftovers away. The Master heard the loud sound of crows cawing, and he asked Venerable Ānanda: “Ānanda, why is there this loud sound of crows cawing?" Venerable Ānanda informed him of what had happened, and the Master said:

“But Ānanda, don’t the monks eat the leftovers from those who are sick?"

“No, Master, they don’t."

The Master then spoke on the Dhamma and addressed the monks: “Monks, I allow you to eat the leftovers from those who are sick and also from those who are not sick. And, monks, this is how food is made leftover: ‘I’ve had enough of all of this.’ And so, monks, this training rule should be recited thus:

Final ruling

7“If a monk who has eaten and expressed his satisfaction eats staple or non-staple food which is not left over, he commits an offence entailing confession."

Definitions

8A: whoever … Monk: … The monk who is ordained by a complete Saṅgha through a procedure of one motion and three announcements, which is unchallengeable and fit to stand; this sort of monk is meant in this case.

9Who has eaten: who has eaten any of the five staple foods, even what is eaten with the tip of a blade of grass.

10Expressed his satisfaction: eating is seen, staple food is seen, it is offered by one standing within arms-reach, a refusal is seen.

11Not left over: the making it leftover is done with what is not allowable; it is done with what has not been received; it is done with what is not held in hand; it is done not within arms-reach; it is done by one who has not finished eating; it is done by one who has finished eating, who has expressed his satisfaction, and who has risen from his seat; ‘I’ve had enough of all of this,’ has not been said; it is not leftover from one who is sick - this is called ‘not leftover.’

12Left over: the making it leftover is done with what is allowable; it is done with what has been received; it is done with what is held in hand; it is done within arms-reach; it is done by one who has finished eating; it is done by one who has finished eating, who has expressed his satisfaction, but who has not risen from his seat; ‘I’ve had enough of all of this,’ has been said; it is leftover from one who is sick - this is called ‘leftover.’

13Non-staple food: apart from the five staple foods, the post-midday allowables, the seven-day allowables, and the lifetime allowables - the rest is called ‘non-staple food.’

14Staple food: there are five kinds of staple food: cooked grain, porridge, flour, fish, and meat.

15If he receives it intending to eat it, he commits an offence of bad conduct. For every mouthful, he commits an offence entailing confession.

Permutations

16If it is not leftover, and he does not perceive it as leftover, and he eats staple or non-staple food, he commits an offence entailing confession. If it is not leftover, but he has doubts about it, and he eats staple or non-staple food, he commits an offence entailing confession. If it is not leftover, but he perceives it as leftover, and he eats staple or non-staple food, he commits an offence entailing confession.

17If he receives post-midday allowables, seven-day allowables, or lifetime allowables for the purpose of food, he commits an offence of bad conduct. For every mouthful, he commits an offence of bad conduct. If it is leftover, but he does not perceive it as leftover, he commits an offence of bad conduct. If it is leftover, but he has doubts about it, he commits an offence of bad conduct. If it is leftover, and he perceives it as leftover, there is no offence.

Non-offences

18There is no offence: if he eats it after getting it made leftover; if he receives it with the intention of getting it made leftover and then eating it; if he goes, taking food for the benefit of another; if he eats the leftovers from a sick person; if, when there is a reason, he makes use of post-midday allowables, seven-day allowables, or lifetime allowables; if he is insane; if he is the first offender.

19The fifth rule, the training rule on satisfaction, is finished.

12p_2V_776Atirittaṁ nāma kappiyakataṁ hoti, paṭiggahitakataṁ hoti, uccāritakataṁ hoti, hatthapāse kataṁ hoti, bhuttāvinā kataṁ hoti, bhuttāvinā pavāritena āsanā avuṭṭhitena kataṁ hoti, "alametaṁ sabban"ti vuttaṁ hoti, gilānātirittaṁ hoti— etaṁ atirittaṁ nāma.

13p_2V_777Khādanīyaṁ nāma pañca bhojanāni— yāmakālikaṁ sattāhakālikaṁ yāvajīvikaṁ ṭhapetvā avasesaṁ khādanīyaṁ nāma.

14p_2V_778Bhojanīyaṁ nāma pañca bhojanāni— odano, kummāso, sattu, maccho, maṁsaṁ.

18p_2V_782, msdiv241Anāpatti— atirittaṁ kārāpetvā bhuñjati, "atirittaṁ kārāpetvā bhuñjissāmī"ti paṭiggaṇhāti, aññassatthāya haranto gacchati, gilānassa sesakaṁ bhuñjati, yāmakālikaṁ sattāhakālikaṁ yāvajīvikaṁ sati paccaye paribhuñjati, ummattakassa, ādikammikassāti.
