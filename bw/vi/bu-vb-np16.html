Bhikkhu Vibhanga - Nissaggiya Pācittiya 16: The Training Rule on Wool.

  • Translated from the Pali by Bhikkhu Brahmali.   (More copyright information)

Origin story

1At one time the Buddha, the Master, was staying at Sāvatthī in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. At that time a monk obtained some wool while walking through the Kosalan country on his way to Sāvatthī. He bound the wool into a bundle with his upper robe, and then went on. Some people who saw that monk mocked him: “Bhante, how much did this cost you? How much will the profit be?" Because he was mocked, he became embarrassed.

When he arrived at Sāvatthī, he threw the wool to the ground. The monks asked him:

“Why do you throw this wool to the ground?"

“Because people mocked me because of this wool."

“But how far have you carried it?"

“Over 40 kilometres."

The monks of few desires … complained and criticised him: “How can a monk carry wool more than 40 kilometres?"

2After criticising that monk in many ways, they informed the Master. … “Is it true, monk, that you carried wool more than 40 kilometres?" “It’s true, Master." The Buddha, the Master, criticised him: “ … Foolish man, how can you carry wool more than 40 kilometres? This will not give rise to confidence in those without it … And, monks, this training rule should be recited thus:

Final ruling

3“If wool becomes available for a monk while travelling, he may receive it if he wishes. If he receives it and there is no one else to carry it, he may carry it by himself for at most 40 kilometres.Three yojanas. The length of a yojana has not been precisely established (perhaps it never was a precise distance), but a reasonable estimate is 8 miles or just under 13 kilometres (see T.W. Rhys Davids: “On the Ancient Coins and Measures of Ceylon", p.16). Thus 3 yojanas is close to 40 km, which seems like a suitably round number given the uncertainty in the estimate. If he carries it further than that, even if there is no one else to carry it, he commits an offence entailing relinquishment and confession."

Definitions

4A monk [ … ] while travelling: one walking on a road.

5If wool becomes available: if it becomes available from a Saṅgha, from a group, from a relative, from a friend, or if rags becomes available, or if it becomes available by means of his own property.

6If he wishes: if he desires, he may receive it.

7If he receives it [ … ] he may carry it by himself for at most 40 kilometres: he may carry it by himself no further than 40 kilometres.

8There is no one else to carry it: there is no other person who can carry it, either a woman or a man, either a householder or a monastic.

9If he carries it further than that, even if there is no one else to carry it: when he goes beyond 40 kilometres with the first foot, there is an offence of bad conduct. When he goes beyond with second foot, he commits an offence entailing relinquishment and confession. If he stands within the 40 kilometre limit, but drops it beyond the 40 kilometre limit, he commits an offence entailing relinquishment and confession. If he places it in the vehicle or among the goods of another without their knowledge, and it goes more than 40 kilometres, it becomes subject to relinquishment.

The wool is to be relinquished to a Saṅgha, a group, or an individual. “And, monks, it is to be relinquished in this way. … To be expanded as in Nissaggiya Pācittiya 1, verses 13 to 17 with appropriate substitutions. … ‘Bhante, this wool which I have carried more than 40 kilometres is to be relinquished. I relinquish it to the Saṅgha.’ … the Saṅgha should give … the Venerables should give … “I give this wool back to the Venerable."

Permutations

10If he goes more than 40 kilometres and he perceives it as more, he commits an offence entailing relinquishment and confession. If he goes more than 40 kilometres, but he has doubt about it, he commits an offence entailing relinquishment and confession. If he goes more than 40 kilometres, but he perceives it as less, he commits an offence entailing relinquishment and confession.

11If he goes less than 40 kilometres, but he perceives it as more, he commits an offence of bad conduct. If he goes less than 40 kilometres, but he has doubt about it, he commits an offence of bad conduct. If he goes less than 40 kilometres and he perceives it as less, there is no offence.

Non-offences

12There is no offence: if he carries it 40 kilometres; if he carries it less than 40 kilometres; if he carries it 40 kilometres and then carries it back; if he goes 40 kilometres with the intention of staying there, but then goes further; if he gets back what was taken from him and then carries it; if he gets back what was given up by him and then carries it; if he get someone else to carry it; if it is a finished article; if he is insane; if he is the first offender.

13The sixth rule, the training rule on wool, is finished.

 

1p_1V_2176, msdiv571Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṁ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tena kho pana samayena aññatarassa bhikkhuno kosalesu janapade sāvatthiṁ gacchantassa antarāmagge eḷakalomāni uppajjiṁsu. Atha kho so bhikkhu tāni eḷakalomāni uttarāsangena bhaṇḍikaṁ bandhitvā agamāsi. Manussā taṁ bhikkhuṁ passitvā uppaṇḍesuṁ— "kittakena te, bhante, kītāni? Kittako udayo bhavissatī"ti? So bhikkhu tehi manussehi uppaṇḍiyamāno manku ahosi.

Atha kho so bhikkhu sāvatthiṁ gantvā tāni eḷakalomāni ṭhitakova āsumbhi. Bhikkhū taṁ bhikkhuṁ etadavocuṁ:

"Kissa tvaṁ, āvuso, imāni eḷakalomāni ṭhitakova āsumbhasī"ti?

"Tathā hi panāhaṁ, āvuso, imesaṁ eḷakalomānaṁ kāraṇā manussehi uppaṇḍito"ti.

"Kīva dūrato pana tvaṁ, āvuso, imāni eḷakalomāni āharī"ti?

"Atirekatiyojanaṁ, āvuso"ti.

Ye te bhikkhū appicchā … pe … te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti: "Kathañhi nāma bhikkhu atirekatiyojanaṁ eḷakalomāni āharissatī"ti.

5p_1V_2180Eḷakalomāni uppajjeyyun'ti uppajjeyyuṁ saṇghato vā gaṇato vā ñātito vā mittato vā paṁsukūlaṁ vā attano vā dhanena.

6p_1V_2181Ākankhamānenā'ti icchamānena paṭiggahetabbāni.

7p_1V_2182Paṭiggahetvā tiyojanaparamaṁ sahatthā haritabbānī'ti tiyojanaparamatā sahatthā haritabbāni.

8p_1V_2183Asante hārake'ti nāñño koci hārako hoti itthī vā puriso vā gahaṭṭho vā pabbajito vā.

Antotiyojane ṭhito bahitiyojanaṁ pāteti, nissaggiyaṁ pācittiyaṁ. Aññassa yāne vā bhaṇḍe vā ajānantassa pakkhipitvā tiyojanaṁ atikkāmeti, nissaggiyāni honti.

10p_1V_2185, msdiv574Atirekatiyojane atirekasaññī atikkāmeti, nissaggiyaṁ pācittiyaṁ. Atirekatiyojane vematiko atikkāmeti, nissaggiyaṁ pācittiyaṁ. Atirekatiyojane ūnakasaññī atikkāmeti, nissaggiyaṁ pācittiyaṁ.

11p_1V_2186 Ūnakatiyojane atirekasaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Ūnakatiyojane vematiko, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Ūnakatiyojane ūnakasaññī, anāpatti.

 

12p_1V_2187, msdiv575Anāpatti— tiyojanaṁ harati, ūnakatiyojanaṁ harati, tiyojanaṁ haratipi, paccāharatipi, tiyojanaṁ vāsādhippāyo gantvā tato paraṁ harati, acchinnaṁ paṭilabhitvā harati, nissaṭṭhaṁ paṭilabhitvā harati, aññaṁ harāpeti katabhaṇḍaṁ, ummattakassa, ādikammikassāti.
