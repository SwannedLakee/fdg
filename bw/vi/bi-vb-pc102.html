Bhikkhuni Vibhanga

Pārājikas Sanghādisesas Nissaggiya Pācittiyas Pācittiyas Pāṭidesanīyas Sekhiyas Adhikaraṇa-Samathās

Bhikkhuni Vibhanga - Pācittiya 102:Bhikkhu's Pc 6 Not to Lie Down in a Building in Which There is a Man.

  • Translated from the Pali by Brahmali Bhikkhu.

Origin story

1At one time the Buddha, the Master, was staying at Sāvatthī in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. At that time, while walking through the Kosalan country on his way to Sāvatthī, one evening Venerable Anuruddha arrived at a certain village. Just then a woman in that village had prepared her guest house. Venerable Anuruddha approached that woman and said: “Sister, if it is not inconvenient for you, I would like to stay in your guest-house for one night."

“Please stay, bhante."

Other travellers also approached that woman and said: “Madam, if it is not troublesome for you, we would like to stay in your guest-house for one night."

“Sirs, an ascetic is already staying there. If he agrees, you may stay."

Those travellers then approached Venerable Anuruddha and said: “Bhante, if it is not troublesome for you, we would like to stay for one night in the guest-house."

“Please stay."

2Now as soon as that woman had seen Venerable Anuruddha she fell in love with him. She then approached him and said: “Bhante, you will not be at ease surrounded by these people. It would be good if I prepared a bed for you in the main house." Venerable Anuruddha consented by remaining silent.

Then, after preparing a bed in the main house, she put on jewellery and perfume, and she approached Venerable Anuruddha and said: “Bhante, you are good-looking and graceful, and so am I. It would be good, bhante, if I were to become your wife." But Venerable Anuruddha remained silent. She said the same a second time, but got no response. And a third time she said: “Bhante, you are good-looking and graceful, and so am I. It would be good, bhante, if you took me and all this property." But Venerable Anuruddha remained silent for a third time. She then threw off her robe, and she walked up and down, stood, sat down, and lay down in front of him. But Venerable Anuruddha controlled his senses and neither looked at nor spoke to her. Then that woman said: “It’s astounding and amazing. Many people send for me with a hundred or a thousand coins. But this ascetic, even when begged by me, does not want me and all this property." After dressing, she fell down with her head at the feet of Venerable Anuruddha and said: “Bhante, I have committed an offence, in that foolishly, confused, and unskilfully I acted in this way. Bhante, please pardon me for my offence so that I may restrain myself in the future."

“Certainly, sister, you have committed an offence, in that foolishly, confused, and unskilfully you acted in this way. But since you acknowledge your offence and you make amends for it in accordance with the Dhamma, I pardon you. For this is called growth in the training of the noble ones, that is, acknowledging an offence, making amends in accordance with the Dhamma, and undertaking restraint for the future."

3The following morning, with her own hands that woman served and satisfied Venerable Anuruddha with various kinds of fine food. When he had eaten and finished his meal, she paid homage to him and sat down to one side. And Venerable Anuruddha instructed, inspired, and gladdened her with a Dhamma talk. She then asked him: “Wonderful, bhante, wonderful! Just as one might set upright what had been overturned, or reveal what had been hidden, or show the way to one who was lost, or bring a lamp into the darkness so that one with eyes might see what is there—even so has the Venerable Anuruddha made the Dhamma clear in many ways. Bhante, I go for refuge to the Master, and to the Dhamma and to the Saṅgha of monks. Please accept me as a lay follower who from today has gone for refuge for life."

4Later, after arriving at Sāvatthī, Venerable Anuruddha informed the monks. The monks of few desires … complained and criticised Venerable Anuruddha: “How can Venerable Anuruddha arrange his sleeping place with a woman?"

5After criticising him in many ways, they informed the Master. … “Is it true, Anuruddha, that do this?" “It’s true, Master." The Buddha, the Master, criticised them: “ … Anuruddha, how can you arrange your sleeping place with a woman? This will not give rise to confidence in those without it … And, monks, this training rule should be recited thus:

Final ruling

6“If a monk uses a sleeping place with a woman, he commits an offence entailing confession."

Definitions

7A: whoever … Monk: … The monk who is ordained by a complete Saṅgha through a procedure of one motion and three announcements, which is unchallengeable and fit to stand; this sort of monk is meant in this case.

8A woman: a human female, not a female spirit, not a female ghost, not a female animal; even a girl born on that very day, all the more an older one.

9With: together.

10Sleeping place: fully roofed, fully walled; mostly roofed, mostly walled.

11Sleeps … in the same sleeping place when the sun has set: if the monk lies down when the woman is already lying down, he commits an offense entailing confession; if the woman lies down when the monk is already lying down, he commits an offence entailing confession; if they both lie down together, he commits an offence entailing confession; if they get up and then lie down again and again, he commits an offence entailing confession.

Permutations

12If it is a woman, and he perceives her as a woman, and he uses a sleeping place with her, he commits an offence entailing confession. If it is a woman, but he has doubts about it, and he uses a sleeping place with her, he commits an offence entailing confession. If it is a woman, but does not perceive her as a woman, and he uses a sleeping place with her, he commits an offence entailing confession.

13If it is half roofed and half walled, he commits an offence of bad conduct. If he uses a sleeping place with a female spirit, with a female ghost, with a paṇḍaka, with a female animal, he commits an offence of bad conduct. If it is not a woman, but he perceives it as a woman, he commits an offence of bad conduct. If it is not a woman, but he has doubts about it, he commits an offence of bad conduct. If it is not a woman, and he does not perceive it as a woman, there is no offence.

Non-offences

href=

14There is no offence: if it is fully roofed but not fully walled; if it is fully walled but not fully roofed; if it is mostly roofed and mostly walled; if the monk sits when the woman is lying down; if the woman sits when the monk is lying down; if they both sit down together; if he is insane; if he is the first offender.

15The sixth rule, the second training rule on a sleeping place together with, is finished.

Ayaṁ pana samaṇo— mayā sāmaṁ yāciyamāno— na icchati mañceva paṭicchituṁ sabbañca sāpateyyan"ti sāṭakaṁ nivāsetvā āyasmato anuruddhassa pādesu sirasā nipatitvā āyasmantaṁ anuruddhaṁ etadavoca: "Accayo maṁ, bhante, accagamā yathābālaṁ yathāmūḷhaṁ yathāakusalaṁ yāhaṁ evamakāsiṁ. "Taggha tvaṁ, bhagini, accayo accagamā yathābālaṁ yathāmūḷhaṁ yathāakusalaṁ yā tvaṁ evamakāsi. Yato ca kho tvaṁ, bhagini, accayaṁ accayato disvā yathādhammaṁ paṭikarosi, taṁ te mayaṁ paṭiggaṇhāma. Vuddhi hesā, bhagini, ariyassa vinaye yo accayaṁ accayato disvā yathādhammaṁ paṭikaroti āyatiñca saṁvaraṁ āpajjatī"ti.

3p_2V_184Atha kho sā itthī tassā rattiyā accayena āyasmantaṁ anuruddhaṁ paṇītena khādanīyena bhojanīyena sahatthā santappetvā sampavāretvā, āyasmantaṁ anuruddhaṁ bhuttāviṁ onītapattapāṇiṁ abhivādetvā ekamantaṁ nisīdi. Ekamantaṁ nisinnaṁ kho taṁ itthiṁ āyasmā anuruddho dhammiyā kathāya sandassesi samādapesi samuttejesi sampahaṁsesi. Atha kho sā itthī— āyasmatā anuruddhena dhammiyā kathāya sandassitā samādapitā samuttejitā sampahaṁsitā— āyasmantaṁ anuruddhaṁ etadavoca: "Abhikkantaṁ, bhante, abhikkantaṁ, bhante. Seyyathāpi, bhante, nikkujjitaṁ vā ukkujjeyya, paṭicchannaṁ vā vivareyya, mūḷhassa vā maggaṁ ācikkheyya, andhakāre vā telapajjotaṁ dhāreyya— ‘cakkhumanto rūpāni dakkhantī’ti; evamevaṁ ayyena anuruddhena anekapariyāyena dhammo pakāsito. Esāhaṁ, bhante, taṁ bhagavantaṁ saraṇaṁ gacchāmi dhammañca bhikkhusaṇghañca. Upāsikaṁ maṁ ayyo dhāretu ajjatagge pāṇupetaṁ saraṇaṁ gatan"ti.
