Vinaya Piṭaka - Monastic Rules

Bhikkhuni Vibhanga

Pācittiya 117:Bhikkhu's Pc 31 Not to Eat Twice Consecutively at a Food Distribution Place.

  • Translated from the Pali by Brahmali Bhikkhu.

Origin story

First sub-story

1At one time the Buddha, the Master, was staying at Sāvatthī in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. At that time a certain association was preparing a meal at a public rest-house not far from Sāvatthī. Then, after dressing in the morning, monks from the group of six took their bowl and robe and entered Sāvatthī for alms. But when they did not get any alms, they went to that public rest-house. Because it was long since they had been there, people served them respectfully.

For a second and third day those monks did the same thing. Then they thought: “We have no business at the monastery, and tomorrow we will just have to come back right here," and so they stayed on and on right there, eating at the rest-house. And members of other sects departed. People then complained and criticised them: “How can these Sakyan ascetics stay on and on, eating at a public rest-house? We don’t prepare the public rest-house food just for them; we prepare it for everyone."

2Monks heard the complaints of those people, and the monks of few desires … complained and criticised those monks: “How can those monks from the group of six stay on and on, eating at a public rest-house?" … “Is it true, monks, that you do this?" “It’s true, Master." The Buddha, the Master, criticised them: “ … Foolish men, how can you stay on and on, eating at a public rest-house? This will not give rise to confidence in those without it … And, monks, this training rule should be recited thus:

Preliminary ruling

3“If a monk eats more than one meal at a public rest-house, he commits an offence entailing confession."

4In this way the Master laid down this training rule for the monks.

Second sub-story

5Soon afterwards Venerable Sāriputta was travelling in the Kosalan country towards Sāvatthī when he came to a public rest-house. Because it was long since he had been there, people served him respectfully. After he had eaten, Venerable Sāriputta became severely ill, and he was not able to leave that rest-house. Then on the second day too those people said to him: “Eat, bhante." But since he knew that the Master had prohibited eating at a public rest-house after staying on and on, and because he was afraid of wrongdoing, he did not accept it. As a consequence he missed his meal.

6When he arrived at Sāvatthī, he informed the monks of what had happened, and they in turn informed the Master. The Master then spoke on the Dhamma and addressed the monks: “Monks, I allow a sick monk to stay on at a public rest-house and eat there." And so, monks, this training rule should be recited thus:

Final ruling

7“If a monk who is not sick eats more than one meal at a public rest-house, he commits an offence entailing confession."

Definitions

8Not sick: he is able to leave that rest-house.

9Sick: he is not able to leave that rest-house.

10Meal at a pubic rest-house: as much as one needs of any of the five staple foods, prepared for the general public, in a hall, under a roof-cover, at the foot of a tree, or in the open. A monk who is not sick may eat there once. If he receives food beyond that with the intention of eating it, he commits an offence of bad conduct. For every mouthful, he commits an offence entailing confession.

Permutations

11If he is not sick, and he perceives himself as not sick, and he eats more than one meal at a public rest-house, he commits an offence entailing confession. If he is not sick, but he has doubts about it, and he eats more than one meal at a public rest-house, he commits an offence entailing confession. If he is not sick, but he perceives himself as sick, and he eats more than one meal at a public rest-house, he commits an offence entailing confession.

12If he is sick, but he perceives himself as not sick, he commits an offence of bad conduct. If he is sick, but he has doubts about it, he commits an offence of bad conduct. If he is sick, and he perceives himself as sick, there is no offence.

Non-offences

13There is no offence: if he is sick; if he is not sick and he eats once; if he eats after leaving and then returning; if he eats after being invited by the owners; if the food is prepared for him; if there is not as much as one needs; if it is anything apart from the five staple foods; if he is insane; if he is the first offender.

14The first rule, the training rule on rest-house meals, is finished.

Chabbaggiyā bhikkhū pubbaṇhasamayaṁ nivāsetvā pattacīvaramādāya sāvatthiṁ piṇḍāya pavisitvā piṇḍaṁ alabhamānā āvasathaṁ agamaṁsu. Manussā— "cirassampi bhadantā āgatā"ti sakkaccaṁ parivisiṁsu.

Atha kho chabbaggiyā bhikkhū dutiyampi divasaṁ … pe … tatiyampi divasaṁ pubbaṇhasamayaṁ nivāsetvā pattacīvaramādāya sāvatthiṁ piṇḍāya pavisitvā piṇḍaṁ alabhamānā āvasathaṁ gantvā bhuñjiṁsu. Atha kho chabbaggiyānaṁ bhikkhūnaṁ etadahosi: "Kiṁ mayaṁ karissāma ārāmaṁ gantvā. Hiyyopi idheva āgantabbaṁ bhavissatī"ti, tattheva anuvasitvā anuvasitvā āvasathapiṇḍaṁ bhuñjanti. Titthiyā apasakkanti. Manussā ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti: "Kathañhi nāma samaṇā sakyaputtiyā anuvasitvā anuvasitvā āvasathapiṇḍaṁ bhuñjissanti. Nayimesaññeva āvasathapiṇḍo paññatto; sabbesaññeva āvasathapiṇḍo paññatto"ti.

2p_2V_665Assosuṁ kho bhikkhū tesaṁ manussānaṁ ujjhāyantānaṁ khiyyantānaṁ vipācentānaṁ. Ye te bhikkhū appicchā … pe … te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti: "Kathañhi nāma chabbaggiyā bhikkhū anuvasitvā anuvasitvā āvasathapiṇḍaṁ bhuñjissantī"ti … pe … "Saccaṁ kira tumhe, bhikkhave, anuvasitvā anuvasitvā āvasathapiṇḍaṁ bhuñjathā"ti? "Saccaṁ, bhagavā"ti. Vigarahi buddho bhagavā … pe … kathañhi nāma tumhe, moghapurisā, anuvasitvā anuvasitvā āvasathapiṇḍaṁ bhuñjissatha. Netaṁ, moghapurisā, appasannānaṁ vā pasādāya … pe … evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṁ sikkhāpadaṁ uddiseyyātha—

 

3p_2V_666"Eko āvasathapiṇḍo bhuñjitabbo. Tato ce uttari bhuñjeyya, pācittiyan" ti.

4p_2V_667Evañcidaṁ bhagavatā bhikkhūnaṁ sikkhāpadaṁ paññattaṁ hoti.

 

5p_2V_668, msdiv204Tena kho pana samayena āyasmā sāriputto kosalesu janapade sāvatthiṁ gacchanto yena aññataro āvasatho tenupasaṅkami. Manussā— "cirassampi thero āgato"ti sakkaccaṁ parivisiṁsu. Atha kho āyasmato sāriputtassa bhuttāvissa kharo ābādho uppajji, nāsakkhi tamhā āvasathā pakkamituṁ. Atha kho te manussā dutiyampi divasaṁ āyasmantaṁ sāriputtaṁ etadavocuṁ: "bhuñjatha, bhante"ti. Atha kho āyasmā sāriputto— "bhagavatā paṭikkhittaṁ anuvasitvā anuvasitvā āvasathapiṇḍaṁ bhuñjitun"ti kukkuccāyanto na paṭiggahesi; chinnabhatto ahosi. Atha kho āyasmā sāriputto sāvatthiṁ gantvā bhikkhūnaṁ etamatthaṁ ārocesi. Bhikkhū bhagavato etamatthaṁ ārocesuṁ.

6p_2V_669Atha kho bhagavā etasmiṁ nidāne etasmiṁ pakaraṇe dhammiṁ kathaṁ katvā bhikkhū āmantesi: "Anujānāmi, bhikkhave, gilānena bhikkhunā anuvasitvā anuvasitvā āvasathapiṇḍaṁ bhuñjituṁ. Evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṁ sikkhāpadaṁ uddiseyyātha—

 

7p_2V_670, msdiv205"Agilānena bhikkhunā eko āvasathapiṇḍo bhuñjitabbo. Tato ce uttari bhuñjeyya, pācittiyan"ti.

 

8p_2V_671, msdiv206Agilāno nāma sakkoti tamhā āvasathā pakkamituṁ.

9p_2V_672Gilāno nāma na sakkoti tamhā āvasathā pakkamituṁ.

10p_2V_673Āvasathapiṇḍo nāma pañcannaṁ bhojanānaṁ aññataraṁ bhojanaṁ— sālāya vā maṇḍape vā rukkhamūle vā ajjhokāse vā anodissa yāvadattho paññatto hoti. Agilānena bhikkhunā sakiṁ bhuñjitabbo. Tato ce uttari "bhuñjissāmī"ti paṭiggaṇhāti, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Ajjhohāre ajjhohāre āpatti pācittiyassa.

 

11p_2V_674, msdiv207Agilāno agilānasaññī tatuttari āvasathapiṇḍaṁ bhuñjati, āpatti pācittiyassa. Agilāno vematiko tatuttari āvasathapiṇḍaṁ bhuñjati, āpatti pācittiyassa. Agilāno gilānasaññī tatuttari āvasathapiṇḍaṁ bhuñjati, āpatti pācittiyassa.

12p_2V_675Gilāno agilānasaññī, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Gilāno vematiko āpatti dukkaṭassa. Gilāno gilānasaññī, anāpatti.

 

13p_2V_676, msdiv208Anāpatti— gilānassa, agilāno sakiṁ bhuñjati, gacchanto, vā āgacchanto vā bhuñjati, sāmikā nimantetvā bhojenti, odissa paññatto hoti, na yāvadattho paññatto hoti, pañca bhojanāni ṭhapetvā sabbattha anāpatti, ummattakassa, ādikammikassāti.

14p_2V_677 Āvasathapiṇḍasikkhāpadaṁ niṭṭhitaṁ paṭhamaṁ.
