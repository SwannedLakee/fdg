Vinaya Pitaka - Monastic Rules

Nissaggiya Pācittiya 12: Limiting the Value of Requested Light Robe Cloth.

  • Translated from the Pali by Brahmali Bhikkhu.

1–2… “ … this rule of training:

3“If a nun is bargaining for a light cloth,lahupāvuraṇa. she may bargain for one (worth) at most two and a half ‘bronzes.’ If she should bargain for one (worth) more than that, there is an offence of expiation involving forfeiture."

4Light cloth means: whatever is a cloth for the hot weather.

5 Is bargaining for means: is asking for.

6She may bargain for one (worth) at most two and a half “bronzes" means: she may bargain for one worth ten kahāpaṇas.

7–9If she should bargain for one (worth) more than that means: … see Bi-NP.11.2. Read a light cloth (worth) at most more than two and a half “bronzes," more than two and a half “bronzes," less than two and a half “bronzes" … there is no offence.

10There is no offence if she bargains for one (worth) at most two and a half “bronzes"; if she bargains for one (worth) at most less than two and a half “bronzes"; … if she is the first wrong-doer.

11The Twelveth is complete.

 

 

12Recited, ladies, are the thirty rulesBuddhaghosa at Vin-a.919says that eighteen (Nissaggiya Pācittiya) rules of training are laid down for both sides, that is for monks and nuns alike. As these have appeared already in the Mahāvibhaṅga, only twelve additional ones need to be included in the Bhikkhunī-Pātimokkha to bring the total of Nissaggiyas for nuns up to thirty. See BD.3, Introduction, p.xxxvii. for offences of expiation involving forfeiture. Concerning them, I ask the ladies: I hope that you are quite pure in this matter? And a second time I ask: I hope that you are quite pure in this matter? And a third time I ask: I hope that you are quite pure in this matter? The ladies are quite pure in this matter; therefore are they silent. Thus do I understand this.

13Told are the thirty offences of expiation involving forfeiture

Atha kho rājā pasenadi kosalo uṇhakāle mahagghaṁ khomaṁ pārupitvā yena thullanandā bhikkhunī tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā thullanandaṁ bhikkhuniṁ abhivādetvā ekamantaṁ nisīdi. Ekamantaṁ nisinnaṁ kho rājānaṁ pasenadiṁ kosalaṁ thullanandā bhikkhunī dhammiyā kathāya sandassesi samādapesi samuttejesi sampahaṁsesi. Atha kho rājā pasenadi kosalo thullanandāya bhikkhuniyā dhammiyā kathāya sandassito samādapito samuttejito sampahaṁsito thullanandaṁ bhikkhuniṁ etadavoca: “vadeyyāsi, ayye, yena attho”ti. “Sace me tvaṁ, mahārāja, dātukāmosi, imaṁ khomaṁ dehī”ti. Atha kho rājā pasenadi kosalo thullanandāya bhikkhuniyā khomaṁ datvā uṭṭhāyāsanā thullanandaṁ bhikkhuniṁ abhivādetvā padakkhiṇaṁ katvā pakkāmi.
