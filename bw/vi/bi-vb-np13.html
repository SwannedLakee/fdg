Vinaya Piṭaka - Monastic Rules

Nissaggiya Pācittiya 13i.e. the Bhikkhu's NP1: Not to keep an extra robe more than ten days at a time.

  • Translated from the Pali by Brahmali Bhikkhu.

Origin story

1At one time the Buddha, the Master, was staying at Sāvatthī in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. At that time some monks belonging to the group of six, thinking: “The Master has prohibited getting blankets made entirely of black wool," added just a little bit of white on the edge and in this way got blankets made entirely of black wool. The monks of few desires … complained and criticised them: “How can those monks belonging to the group of six add just a little bit of white on the edge and in this way get blankets made entirely of black wool?"

2After criticising those monks in many ways, they informed the Master. … “Is it true, monks, that you add just a little bit of white on the edge and in this way get blankets made entirely of black wool?" “It’s true, Master." The Buddha, the Master, criticised them: “ … Foolish men, how can you add just a little bit of white on the edge and in this way get blankets made entirely of black wool? This will not give rise to confidence in those without it … And, monks, this training rule should be recited thus:

Final ruling

3“If a monk is getting a new blanket made, he should use two parts of entirely black wool, a third part of white, and a fourth part of brown. If he gets a new blanket made without using two parts of entirely black wool, a third part of white, and a fourth part of brown, he commits an offence entailing relinquishment and confession."

Definitions

4New: newly made.

5Blanket: it is made by spreading out, not by weaving.

6Getting made: making or getting made.

7He should use two parts of entirely black wool: having weighed it, he should use two measures.

8A third part of white: one measure of white.

9A fourth part of brown: one measure of brown.

10Without using two parts of entirely black wool, a third part of white, and a fourth part of brown: in the act of making it, or getting it made, without using two measures of entirely black wool, one measure of white, and one measure of brown, he commits an offence of bad conduct. When he gets the blanket, it becomes subject to relinquishment.

The blanket is to be relinquished to a Saṅgha, a group, or an individual. “And, monks, it is to be relinquished in this way. … To be expanded as in Relinquishment 1, MS paragraphs 1873–1877, with appropriate substitutions. … ‘Bhante, this blanket which I got made without using two measures of entirely black wool, one measure of white, and one measure of brown, is to be relinquished.

I relinquish it to the Saṅgha.’ … the Saṅgha should give … the Venerables should give … “I give this blanket back to the Venerable."

Permutations

11If he finishes what he began himself, he commits an offence entailing relinquishment and confession. If others finish what he began himself, he commits an offence entailing relinquishment and confession. If he finishes himself what was begun by others, he commits an offence entailing relinquishment and confession. If others finish what was begun by others, he commits an offence entailing relinquishment and confession.

12If he makes one, or gets one made, for someone else, he commits an offence of bad conduct. If he obtains one that was made by someone else and then uses it, he commits an offence of bad conduct.

Non-offences

13There is no offence: if he makes one using one measure of white and one measure of brown; if he makes on using more than one measure of white and/or more than one measure of brown; if he makes one using just white and/or just brown; if he makes a canopy, a carpet, a screen-wall, a mattress, or a pillow; if he is insane; if he is the first offender.

14The third rule, the training rule on two parts, is finished.

"Saccaṁ, bhagavā"ti. Vigarahi buddho bhagavā … pe … kathañhi nāma tumhe, moghapurisā, thokaññeva odātaṁ ante ādiyitvā tatheva suddhakāḷakānaṁ eḷakalomānaṁ santhataṁ kārāpessatha. 3p_1V_2123"Navaṁ pana bhikkhunā santhataṁ kārayamānena dve bhāgā suddhakāḷakānaṁ eḷakalomānaṁ ādātabbā tatiyaṁ odātānaṁ catutthaṁ gocariyānaṁ. 4p_1V_2124 Navaṁ nāma karaṇaṁ upādāya vuccati.

5p_1V_2125Santhataṁ nāma santharitvā kataṁ hoti avāyimaṁ.

6p_1V_2126Kārayamānenā'ti karonto vā kārāpento vā.

7p_1V_2127Dve bhāgā suddhakāḷakānaṁ eḷakalomānaṁ ādātabbā'ti dhārayitvā dve tulā ādātabbā.

8p_1V_2128Tatiyaṁ odātānan'ti tulaṁ odātānaṁ.

9p_1V_2129Catutthaṁ gocariyānan'ti tulaṁ gocariyānaṁ.

10p_1V_2130Anādā ce bhikkhu dve bhāge suddhakāḷakānaṁ eḷakalomānaṁ tatiyaṁ odātānaṁ catutthaṁ gocariyānan ti. Anādiyitvā dve tule suddhakāḷakānaṁ eḷakalomānaṁ tulaṁ odātānaṁ tulaṁ gocariyānaṁ navaṁ santhataṁ karoti vā kārāpeti vā payoge dukkaṭaṁ, paṭilābhena nissaggiyaṁ hoti.

Nissajjitabbaṁ saṅghassa vā gaṇassa vā puggalassa vā. Evañca pana, bhikkhave, nissajjitabbaṁ … pe … "Idaṁ me, bhante, santhataṁ anādiyitvā dve tule suddhakāḷakānaṁ eḷakalomānaṁ tulaṁ odātānaṁ tulaṁ gocariyānaṁ kārāpitaṁ nissaggiyaṁ.

Imāhaṁ saṅghassa nissajjāmī"ti … pe … dadeyyāti … pe … dadeyyunti … pe … āyasmato dammīti.

 

11p_1V_2131 Attanā vippakataṁ attanā pariyosāpeti, nissaggiyaṁ pācittiyaṁ. Attanā vippakataṁ parehi pariyosāpeti, nissaggiyaṁ pācittiyaṁ. Parehi vippakataṁ attanā pariyosāpeti, nissaggiyaṁ pācittiyaṁ. Parehi vippakataṁ parehi pariyosāpeti, nissaggiyaṁ pācittiyaṁ.

12p_1V_2132Aññassatthāya karoti vā kārāpeti vā, āpatti dukkaṭassa. Aññena kataṁ paṭilabhitvā paribhuñjati, āpatti dukkaṭassa.

 

13p_1V_2133 Anāpatti— tulaṁ odātānaṁ tulaṁ gocariyānaṁ ādiyitvā karoti, bahutaraṁ odātānaṁ bahutaraṁ gocariyānaṁ ādiyitvā karoti, suddhaṁ odātānaṁ suddhaṁ gocariyānaṁ ādiyitvā karoti, vitānaṁ vā bhūmattharaṇaṁ vā sāṇipākāraṁ vā bhisiṁ vā bibbohanaṁ vā karoti, ummattakassa, ādikammikassāti.
