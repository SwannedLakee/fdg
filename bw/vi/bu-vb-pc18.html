Vinaya Pitaka - Monastic Rules

Bhikkhu Vibhanga - Pācittiya 18: The Training Rule on Upper Stories.

  • Translated from the Pali by Bhikkhu Brahmali.   (More copyright information)

Origin story

1At one time the Buddha, the Master, was staying at Sāvatthī in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. At that time two monks were staying in a dwelling belonging to the Saṅgha which had an upper storey, one staying below and one above. The monk above sat down hastily on a bed with detachable legs. The legs fell off and hit the monk below on the head, and he cried out. Monks came rushing to and asked him: “Why did you cry out?" And he informed them of what had happened.

The monks of few desires … complained and criticised them: “How can a monk sit down hastily on a bed with detachable legs on an upper storey in a dwelling belonging to the Saṅgha?"

2After criticising that monk in many ways, they informed the Master. … “Is it true, monk, that you do this?" “It’s true, Master." The Buddha, the Master, criticised him: “ … Foolish man, how can you sit down hastily on a bed with detachable legs on a upper storey in a dwelling belonging to the Saṅgha? This will not give rise to confidence in those without it … And, monks, this training rule should be recited thus:

Final ruling

3“If a monk, in a dwelling belonging to the Saṅgha, sits down or lies down on a bed or a bench with detachable legs on an upper storey, he commits an offence entailing confession."

Definitions

4A: whoever … Monk: … The monk who is ordained by a complete Saṅgha through a procedure of one motion and three announcements, which is unchallengeable and fit to stand; this sort of monk is meant in this case.

5Belonging to the Saṅgha: a dwelling given to the Saṅgha, given up to the Saṅgha.

6Upper storey: a man of average height does not hit his head.

7A bed with detachable legs: inserting the limbs, it stands.

8A bench with detachable legs: inserting the limbs, it stands.

9Sits down: if he sits down on it, he commits an offence entailing confession.

10Lies down: if he lies down on it, he commits an offence entailing confession

Permutations

11If it belongs to the Order, and he perceives it as belonging to the Order, and he sits down or lies down on a bed or a bench with detachable legs on the upper story, he commits an offense entailing confession. If it belongs to the Order, but he is unsure if it does … If it belongs to the Order, but he perceives it as belonging to an individual, and he sits down or lies down on a bed or a bench with detachable legs on the upper story, he commits an offense entailing confession.

12If it belongs to an individual, but he perceives it as belonging to the Saṅgha, he commits an offence of bad conduct. If it belongs to an individual, but has doubt about it, he commits an offence of bad conduct.

If it belongs to an individual, and he perceives it as belonging to an individual, but that individual is not himself, he commits an offence of bad conduct. If it belongs to himself, there is no offence.

Non-offences

13There is no offence: if there is no upper storey; if the upper storey is so low that one hits the head; if the lower storey is not in use; if the upper storey has floorboards; if bolts to fasten the legs have been given; if he stands on it to get hold of something or to put something up; if he is insane; if he is the first offender.

14The eighth rule, the training rule on upper stories, is finished.

Atha kho so bhikkhu bhikkhūnaṁ etamatthaṁ ārocesi.

Ye te bhikkhū appicchā … pe … te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti: "Kathañhi nāma bhikkhu saṇghike vihāre uparivehāsakuṭiyā āhaccapādakaṁ mañcaṁ sahasā abhinisīdissatī"ti.
