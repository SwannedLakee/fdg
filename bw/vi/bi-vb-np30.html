Vinaya Pitaka - Monastic Rules

Nissaggiya Pācittiya 30i.e. the Bhikkhu's NP30: Not to divert for her own benefit a donation made to the Saṇgha.

  • Translated from the Pali by Brahmali Bhikkhu.

Origin story

1At one time the Buddha, the Master, was staying at Sāvatthī in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. At that time an association had prepared a meal together with robes for the Saṅgha, thinking: “After giving the food, we will offer the robes."

Then some monks belonging to the group of six went to that association and said: “Sirs, give these robes to us."

“Bhantes, we will not give them to you. We have prepared our annual alms-offering together with robes for the Saṅgha."

“The Saṅgha has many donors and supporters. But since we are staying here, we depend on you and look to you for support. If you don’t give to us, then who will? So give us these robes." Being pressured by those monks belonging to the group of six, that association gave the prepared robes to them and served the food to the Saṅgha.

Those monks who knew that a meal together with robes had been prepared for the Saṅgha, but who did not know that the robes had been given to those monks belonging to the group six, said: “You may offer the robes."

“Bhantes, there aren’t any. The robes we had prepared were diverted by the Venerables belonging to the group of six to themselves."

The monks of few desires … complained and criticised those monks: “How can those monks belonging to the group of six divert to themselves things they know are intended for the Saṅgha?"

2After criticising those monks in many ways, they informed the Master. … “Is it true, monks, that you divert to yourselves things you know are intended for the Saṅgha?" “It’s true, Master." The Buddha, the Master, criticised them: “ … Foolish men, how can you divert to yourselves things that you know are intended for the Saṅgha? This will not give rise to confidence in those without it … And, monks, this training rule should be recited thus:

Final ruling

3“If a monk diverts to himself something that he knows was intended for the Saṅgha, he commits an offence entailing relinquishment and confession."

Definitions

4A: whoever … Monk: … The monk who is ordained by a complete Saṅgha through a procedure of one motion and three announcements, which is unchallengeable and fit to stand; this sort of monk is meant in this case.

5He knows: he knows by himself or others have informed him or he has found out by making enquiries.

6For the Saṅgha: it is given to the Saṅgha, relinquished.

7Something: robes, almsfood, lodging requisites, and medicines; even a piece of chunam, a toothwood, or a piece of string.

8Intended: they have said: “We will give; we will make."

9In the act of diverting it to himself, he commits an offence of bad conduct. When he gets it, it becomes subject to relinquishment. It is to be relinquished to a Saṅgha, a group, or an individual. “And, monks, it is to be relinquished in this way. … To be expanded as in Nissaggiya Pācittiya 1, verses 13 to 17 with appropriate substitutions. … ‘Bhante, this thing which I diverted to myself knowing that it was intended for the Saṅgha is to be relinquished. I relinquish it to the Saṅgha.’ … the Saṅgha should give … the Venerables should give … “I give this back to the Venerable."

Permutations

10If it is intended for the Saṅgha and he perceives it as such, and he diverts it to himself, he commits an offence entailing relinquishment and confession./p>

11If it is intended for the Saṅgha, but he has doubts about it, and he diverts it to himself, he commits an offence entailing relinquishment and confession. If it is intended for the Saṅgha, but he does not perceives it as such, and he diverts it to himself, he commits an offence entailing relinquishment and confession.

If it is intended for one Saṅgha and he diverts it to another Saṅgha or to a shrine, he commits an offence of bad conduct. If it is intended for one shrine and he diverts it to another shrine or to a Saṅgha or to an individual, he commits an offence of bad conduct. If it is intended for an individual and he diverts it to another individual or to a Saṅgha or to a shrine, he commits an offence of bad conduct.

If it is not intended for the Saṅgha, but he perceives it as intended for the Saṅgha, he commits an offence of bad conduct. If it is not intended for the Saṅgha, but he has doubts about it, he commits an offence of bad conduct. If it is not intended for the Saṅgha and he perceives it as not intended for the Saṅgha, there is no offence.

Non-offences

12There is no offence: if being asked: “Where may we give?", he says: “Give where your gift goes towards requisites or repairs or where it will last for a long time, or give where you feel inspired"; if he is insane; if he is the first offender.

13The tenth rule, the training rule
on what is intended, is finished.
The third chapter
on bowls is finished.

14This is the summary:

15Two on bowls, and tonics,
Rainy season, the fifth on a gift;
Oneself, getting woven, haste
Dangerous, and with the Saṅgha.

16“Venerables, the thirty rules entailing relinquishment and confession have been recited. In regard to this I ask the Venerables: ‘Are you pure in this?’ A second time I ask: ‘Are you pure in this?’ A third time I ask: ‘Are you pure in this?’ You are pure in this and therefore silent. I will remember it thus."

17The chapter on relinquishment is finished.

18The text on expulsion is finished.

 

1p_1V_2421 Tena samayena buddho bhagavā sāvatthiyaṁ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tena kho pana samayena sāvatthiyaṁ aññatarassa pūgassa sanghassa sacīvarabhattaṁ paṭiyattaṁ hoti— "bhojetvā cīvarena acchādessāmā"ti.

Atha kho chabbaggiyā bhikkhū yena so pūgo tenupasankamiṁsu; upasankamitvā taṁ pūgaṁ etadavocuṁ: "dethāvuso, amhākaṁ imāni cīvarānī"ti.

"Na mayaṁ, bhante, dassāma. Amhākaṁ sanghassa anuvassaṁ sacīvarabhikkhā paññattā"ti.

"Bahū, āvuso, sanghassa dāyakā, bahū sanghassa bhattā. Mayaṁ tumhe nissāya tumhe sampassantā idha viharāma. Tumhe ce amhākaṁ na dassatha, atha ko carahi amhākaṁ dassati? Dethāvuso, amhākaṁ imāni cīvarānī"ti. Atha kho so pūgo chabbaggiyehi bhikkhūhi nippīḷiyamāno yathāpaṭiyattaṁ cīvaraṁ chabbaggiyānaṁ bhikkhūnaṁ datvā saṇghaṁ bhattena parivisi.

Ye te bhikkhū jānanti sanghassa sacīvarabhattaṁ paṭiyattaṁ, na ca jānanti chabbaggiyānaṁ bhikkhūnaṁ dinnanti, te evamāhaṁsu— "oṇojethāvuso, sanghassa cīvaran"ti.

"Natthi, bhante. Yathāpaṭiyattaṁ cīvaraṁ ayyā chabbaggiyā attano pariṇāmesun"ti.

Ye te bhikkhū appicchā … pe … te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti: "Kathañhi nāma chabbaggiyā bhikkhū jānaṁ saṇghikaṁ lābhaṁ pariṇataṁ attano pariṇāmessantī"ti.
