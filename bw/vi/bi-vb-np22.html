Vinaya Piṭaka - Monastic Rules

Nissaggiya Pācittiya 22i.e. the Bhikkhu's NP19: The Training Rule on Trading With Money.

  • Translated from the Pali by Brahmali Bhikkhu.

Origin story

1At one time the Buddha, the Master, was staying at Sāvatthī in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. At that time some monks belonging to the group of six engaged in various kinds of trade with money. People grumbled and complained: “How can the Sakyan ascetics engage in various kinds of trade with money, just like householders who indulge in sensual pleasures?"

2Monks heard the complaints of those people, and the monks of few desires … complained and criticised those monks: “How can those monks belonging to the group of six engage in various kinds of trade with money?"

3After criticising those monks in many ways, they informed the Master. … “Is it true, monks, that you engage in various kinds of trade with money?" “It’s true, Master." The Buddha, the Master, criticised them: “ … Foolish men, how can you engage in various kinds of trade with money? This will not give rise to confidence in those without it … And, monks, this training rule should be recited thus:

Final ruling

4“If a monk engages in various kinds of trade with money, he commits an offence entailing relinquishment and confession."

Definitions

5A: whoever … Monk: … The monk who is ordained by a complete Saṅgha through a procedure of one motion and three announcements, which is unchallengeable and fit to stand; this sort of monk is meant in this case.

6Various kinds: what is shaped, what is not shaped, and what is both shaped and not shaped. What is shaped: what is meant for the head, what is meant for the neck, what is meant for the hands, what is meant for the feet, what is meant for the waist. What is not shaped: what is shaped in a lump is so called. What is both shaped and not shaped: both of them.

7Money: a golden kahāpaṇa, a copper māsaka, a wooden māsaka, a resin māsaka, whatever is used in commerce.

8Engages in: if he exchanges what is shaped with what is shaped, he commits an offence entailing relinquishment and confession. If he exchanges what is not shaped with what is shaped, he commits an offence entailing relinquishment and confession. If he exchanges what is both shaped and not shaped with what is shaped, he commits an offence entailing relinquishment and confession.

If he exchanges what is shaped with what is not shaped, he commits an offence entailing relinquishment and confession. If he exchanges what is not shaped with what is not shaped, he commits an offence entailing relinquishment and confession. If he exchanges what is both shaped and not shaped with what is not shaped, he commits an offence entailing relinquishment and confession.

If he exchanges what is shaped with what is both shaped and not shaped, he commits an offence entailing relinquishment and confession. If he exchanges what is not shaped with what is both shaped and not shaped, he commits an offence entailing relinquishment and confession. If he exchanges what is both shaped and not shaped with what is both shaped and not shaped, he commits an offence entailing relinquishment and confession.

It is to be relinquished in the midst of the Saṅgha. “And, monks, it is to be relinquished in this way. After approaching the Saṅgha, that monk should put his upper robe over one shoulder and pay homage at the feet of the senior monks. He should then squat on his haunches, put the palms of his hands together, and say: ‘Bhante, I have engaged in various kinds of trade with money. This is to be relinquished. I relinquish it to the Saṅgha.’" After relinquishing it, he is to confess the offence. The confession should be received by an experienced and competent monk.

If a monastery attendant or a lay disciple is available, he should be told: “Look into this." If he says: “What can I get you with this?", one should not say: “Get this or that;" one should point out what is allowable: ghee, oil, honey, or molasses. If he makes a purchase and brings back what is allowable, everyone may enjoy it except the one who exchanged into money.

If this is what happens, it is well. If not, he should be told: “Discard this." If he discards it, it is well. If he does not, a monk who has five qualities is to be chosen as the money-discarder: one who is not swayed by desire, anger, delusion, or fear, and who knows what discarding and not discarding refer to.

“And, monks, this is how he should be chosen. First the monk should be asked and then an experienced and competent monk should inform the Saṅgha:

9“‘Bhante, let the Saṅgha listen to me. If it seems appropriate to the Saṅgha, it should choose monk so-and-so as the money-discarder. This is the motion.

10“‘Bhante, let the Saṅgha listen to me. The Saṅgha chooses monk so-and-so as the money-discarder. Any monk who agrees to choosing monk so-and-so as the money-discarder should remain silent. Any monk who does not agree should say so.

11“‘The Saṅgha has chosen monks so-and-so as the money-discarder. The Saṅgha approves and is therefore silent. I will remember it thus.’"

12The chosen monk should drop it without taking note. If he takes note of where he drops it, he commits an offence of bad conduct.

Permutations

13If it is money, and he perceives it as money, and he exchanges it into money, he commits an offence entailing relinquishment and confession. If it is money, but he has doubt about it, and he exchanges it into money, he commits an offence entailing relinquishment and confession. If it is money, but he does not perceive it as money, and he exchanges it into money, he commits an offence entailing relinquishment and confession.

If it is not money, but he perceives it as money, and he exchanges it into money, he commits an offence entailing relinquishment and confession. If it is not money, but he has doubt about it, and he exchanges it into money, he commits an offence entailing relinquishment and confession. If it is not money, and he does not perceive it as money, but he exchanges it into money, he commits an offence entailing relinquishment and confession.

14If it is not money, but he perceives it as money, he commits an offence of bad conduct. If it is not money, but he has doubt about it, he commits an offence of bad conduct. If it is not money, and he does not perceive it as money, there is no offence.

Non-offences

15There is no offence: if he is insane; if he is the first offender.

16The ninth rule, the training rule on trading with money, is finished.

4p_1V_2225"Yo pana bhikkhu nānappakārakaṁ rūpiyasaṁvohāraṁ samāpajjeyya, nissaggiyaṁ pācittiyan"ti. (19:38)

6p_1V_2227Nānappakārakaṁ nāma katampi akatampi katākatampi. Kataṁ nāma sīsūpagaṁ gīvūpagaṁ hatthūpagaṁ pādūpagaṁ kaṭūpagaṁ. Akataṁ nāma ghanakataṁ vuccati. Katākataṁ nāma tadubhayaṁ.

7p_1V_2228Rūpiyaṁ nāma satthuvaṇṇo kahāpaṇo, lohamāsako, dārumāsako, jatumāsako ye vohāraṁ gacchanti.

8p_1V_2229Samāpajjeyyā'ti katena kataṁ cetāpeti, nissaggiyaṁ pācittiyaṁ. Katena akataṁ cetāpeti, nissaggiyaṁ pācittiyaṁ. Katena katākataṁ cetāpeti, nissaggiyaṁ pācittiyaṁ.

Akatena kataṁ cetāpeti, nissaggiyaṁ pācittiyaṁ. Akatena akataṁ cetāpeti, nissaggiyaṁ pācittiyaṁ. Akatena katākataṁ cetāpeti, nissaggiyaṁ pācittiyaṁ.

Katākatena kataṁ cetāpeti, nissaggiyaṁ pācittiyaṁ. Katākatena akataṁ cetāpeti, nissaggiyaṁ pācittiyaṁ. Katākatena katākataṁ cetāpeti, nissaggiyaṁ pācittiyaṁ.

Saṇghamajjhe nissajjitabbaṁ. Evañca pana, bhikkhave, nissajjitabbaṁ. Tena bhikkhunā saṇghaṁ upasaṅkamitvā ekaṁsaṁ uttarāsaṅgaṁ karitvā vuḍḍhānaṁ bhikkhūnaṁ pāde vanditvā ukkuṭikaṁ nisīditvā añjaliṁ paggahetvā evamassa vacanīyo: "Ahaṁ, bhante, nānappakārakaṁ rūpiyasaṁvohāraṁ samāpajjiṁ. Idaṁ me nissaggiyaṁ. Imāhaṁ saṅghassa nissajjāmī"ti. Nissajjitvā āpatti desetabbā. Byattena bhikkhunā paṭibalena āpatti paṭiggahetabbā.

Sace tattha āgacchati ārāmiko vā upāsako vā so vattabbo— "āvuso, imaṁ jānāhī"ti. Sace so bhaṇati— "iminā kiṁ āhariyyatū"ti, na vattabbo— "imaṁ vā imaṁ vā āharā"ti. Kappiyaṁ ācikkhitabbaṁ— sappi vā telaṁ vā madhu vā phāṇitaṁ vā. Sace so tena parivattetvā kappiyaṁ āharati, rūpiyacetāpakaṁ ṭhapetvā, sabbeheva paribhuñjitabbaṁ.

Evañcetaṁ labhetha, iccetaṁ kusalaṁ; no ce labhetha, so vattabbo— "āvuso, imaṁ chaḍḍehī"ti. Sace so chaḍḍeti, iccetaṁ kusalaṁ; no ce chaḍḍeti, pañcahaṅgehi samannāgato bhikkhu rūpiyachaḍḍako sammannitabbo— yo na chandāgatiṁ gaccheyya, na dosāgatiṁ gaccheyya, na mohāgatiṁ gaccheyya, na bhayāgatiṁ gaccheyya, chaḍḍitāchaḍḍitañca jāneyya.

"Evañca pana, bhikkhave, sammannitabbo. Paṭhamaṁ bhikkhu yācitabbo. Yācitvā byattena bhikkhunā paṭibalena saṅgho ñāpetabbo—

9p_1V_2230 ‘Suṇātu me, bhante, saṅgho. Yadi saṅghassa pattakallaṁ, saṅgho itthannāmaṁ bhikkhuṁ rūpiyachaḍḍakaṁ sammanneyya. Esā ñatti.

12p_1V_2233Tena sammatena bhikkhunā animittaṁ katvā pātetabbaṁ. Sace nimittaṁ katvā pāteti, āpatti dukkaṭassa.
