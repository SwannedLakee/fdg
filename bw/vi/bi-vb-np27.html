Bhikkhuni Vibhanga - Nissaggiya Pācittiya 27i.e. the Bhikkhu's NP26: The Training Rule on Asking for Thread.

  • Translated from the Pali by Brahmali Bhikkhu.

Origin story

1At one time the Buddha, the Master, was staying at Rājagaha in the squirrel sanctuary in the Bamboo Grove. At that time some monks belonging to the group of six asked for much thread during the robe making season. And even when their robes had been sewn, there was much thread left over. And those monks thought: “Well, let’s ask for more thread and get weavers to weave us robes." But even when the robes had been woven, there was much thread left over. For a second time those monks asked for more thread and got weavers to weave them robes. Once again, when the robes had been woven, there was much thread left over. For a third time those monks asked for more thread and got weavers to weave them robes. People grumbled and complained: “How can the Sakyan ascetics ask for thread and then get weavers to weave them robes?"

2Monks heard the complaints of those people, and the monks of few desires … complained and criticised those monks: “How can those monks belonging to the group of six ask for thread and then get weavers to weave them robes?"

3After criticising those monks in many ways, they informed the Master. … “Is it true, monks, that you ask for thread and then get weavers to weave you robes?" “It’s true, Master." The Buddha, the Master, criticised them: “ … Foolish men, how can you ask for thread and then get weavers to weave you robes? This will not give rise to confidence in those without it … And, monks, this training rule should be recited thus:

Final ruling

4“If a monk asks for thread himself, and then gets weavers to weave him robe-cloth, he commits an offence entailing relinquishment and confession."

Definitions

5A: whoever … Monk: … The monk who is ordained by a complete Saṅgha through a procedure of one motion and three announcements, which is unchallengeable and fit to stand; this sort of monk is meant in this case.

6Himself: he himself has asked.

7Thread: there are six kinds of thread: linen, cotton, silk, wool, jute, and hemp.

8Weavers: for every action to get it woven by weavers, he commits an offence of bad conduct. When he gets the robe, it becomes subject to relinquishment.

The robe-cloth is to be relinquished to a Saṅgha, a group, or an individual. with appropriate substitutions. … ‘Bhante, this robe-cloth which I got woven by weavers after asking for the thread myself is to be relinquished. I relinquish it to the Saṅgha.’ … the Saṅgha should give … the Venerables should give … “I give this robe-cloth back to the Venerable."

Permutations

9If he got it woven, and he perceives that he did, he commits an offence entailing relinquishment and confession. If he got it woven, but he has doubts about it, he commits an offence entailing relinquishment and confession. If he got it woven, but he perceives that he did not, he commits an offence entailing relinquishment and confession.

10If he did not get it woven, but he perceives that he did, he commits an offence of bad conduct. If he did not get it woven, but he has doubts about it, he commits an offence of bad conduct. If he did not get it woven, and he perceives that he did not, there is no offence.

Non-offences

11There is no offence: if he sews a robe; if it is a bandage; if it is a waistband; if it is a shoulder-strap; if it is a bowl-bag; if it is a water-strainer; if it is from relatives; if it is from those who have given an invitation; if it for the benefit of someone else; if it is by means of one’s own property; if he is insane; if he is the first offender.

12The sixth rule, the training rule on asking for thread, is finished.

Katepi cīvare bahuṁ suttaṁ avasiṭṭhaṁ hoti. Atha kho chabbaggiyānaṁ bhikkhūnaṁ etadahosi: "handa mayaṁ, āvuso, aññampi suttaṁ viññāpetvā tantavāyehi cīvaraṁ vāyāpemā"ti. Atha kho chabbaggiyā bhikkhū aññampi suttaṁ viññāpetvā tantavāyehi cīvaraṁ vāyāpesuṁ. Vītepi cīvare bahuṁ suttaṁ avasiṭṭhaṁ hoti. Dutiyampi kho chabbaggiyā bhikkhū aññampi suttaṁ viññāpetvā tantavāyehi cīvaraṁ vāyāpesuṁ. Vītepi cīvare bahuṁ suttaṁ avasiṭṭhaṁ hoti. Tatiyampi kho chabbaggiyā bhikkhū aññampi suttaṁ viññāpetvā tantavāyehi cīvaraṁ vāyāpesuṁ. Manussā ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti: "Kathañhi nāma samaṇā sakyaputtiyā sāmaṁ suttaṁ viññāpetvā tantavāyehi cīvaraṁ vāyāpessantī"ti.

2p_1V_2351Assosuṁ kho bhikkhū tesaṁ manussānaṁ ujjhāyantānaṁ khiyyantānaṁ vipācentānaṁ. Ye te bhikkhū appicchā … pe … te ujjhāyanti khiyyanti vipācenti: "Kathañhi nāma chabbaggiyā bhikkhū sāmaṁ suttaṁ viññāpetvā tantavāyehi cīvaraṁ vāyāpessantī"ti.

3p_1V_2352Atha kho te bhikkhū chabbaggiye bhikkhū anekapariyāyena vigarahitvā bhagavato etamatthaṁ ārocesuṁ … pe … "Saccaṁ kira tumhe, bhikkhave, sāmaṁ suttaṁ viññāpetvā tantavāyehi cīvaraṁ vāyāpethā"ti? "Saccaṁ, bhagavā"ti. Vigarahi buddho bhagavā … pe … kathañhi nāma tumhe, moghapurisā, sāmaṁ suttaṁ viññāpetvā tantavāyehi cīvaraṁ vāyāpessatha. Netaṁ, moghapurisā, appasannānaṁ vā pasādāya … pe … evañca pana, bhikkhave, imaṁ sikkhāpadaṁ uddiseyyātha—
