Vinaya Pitaka - Monastic Rules

Bhikkhuni Vibhanga

Pācittiya 112:Bhikkhu's Pc 16 Not to Set a Bhikkhuni Apart in Order to Make Her Leave.

  • Translated from the Pali by Brahmali Bhikkhu.

Origin story

1At one time the Buddha was staying at Sāvatthī in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s Monastery. At that time some monks belonging to the group of six took possession of the best sleeping places, but the senior monks made them move. They then thought: “Through what strategy may we dwell right here during the rainy season?" Then they arranged their sleeping places in a way that encroached on the senior monks, thinking: “Those who feel crowded will leave."

The monks of few desires … complained and criticised them: “How can those monks belonging to the group of six arrange their sleeping places in a way that encroaches upon the senior monks?"

2After criticising those monks in many ways, in those without it … And, monks, this training rule should be recited thus:

Final ruling

3“If, in a dwelling belonging to the Saṅgha, a monk arranges his sleeping place in a way that encroaches on a monk that he knows arrived there before him, with the intention that anyone who feels crowded will leave, and he does so only for this reason and no other, he commits an offence entailing confession."

Definitions

4A: whoever … Monk: … The monk who is ordained by a complete Saṅgha through a procedure of one motion and three announcements, which is unchallengeable and fit to stand; this sort of monk is meant in this case.

5Belonging to the Saṅgha: a dwelling given to the Saṅgha, given up to the Saṅgha.

6He knows: he knows that he is senior, he knows that he is sick, he knows that it was given to him by the Saṅgha.

7Encroaches on: enters after.

8Arranges his sleeping place: if he puts out his sleeping place in the vicinity of the bed, the bench, the entry way, or the departure way, he commits an offence of bad conduct. If he sits down or lies down on it, he commits an offence entailing confession.

9He does so only for this reason and no other: there is no other reason for arranging the sleeping place in a way that encroaches.

Permutations

10If it belongs to the Saṅgha, and he perceives it as belonging to the Saṅgha, and he arranges his sleeping place in a way that encroaches, he commits an offence entailing confession. If it belongs to the Saṅgha, but he has doubts about it, and he arranges his sleeping place in a way that encroaches, he commits an offence entailing confession. If it belongs to the Saṅgha, but he perceives it as belonging to an individual, and he arranges his sleeping place in a way that encroaches, he commits an offence entailing confession.

11If he puts out his sleeping place, or get it put out, anywhere apart from the vicinity of the bed, the bench, the entry way, or the departure way, he commits an offence of bad conduct. If he sits down or lies down on it, he commits an offence of bad conduct. If he puts out his sleeping place, or gets it put out, in the vicinity of a dwelling, in an assembly-hall, under a roof-cover, at the foot of a tree, or in the open, he commits an offence of bad conduct. If he sits down or lies down on it, he commits an offence of bad conduct.

If it belongs to an individual, but he perceives it as belonging to the Saṅgha, he commits an offence of bad conduct. If it belongs to an individual, but has doubt about it, he commits an offence of bad conduct. If it belongs to an individual, and he perceives it as belonging to an individual, but that individual is not himself, he commits an offence of bad conduct. If it belongs to himself, there is no offence.

Non-offences

12There is no offence: if he enters because he is sick; if he enters because he is feeling cold or hot; if there are dangers; if he is insane; if he is the first offender.

13The sixth rule, the training rule on encroaching, is finished.
